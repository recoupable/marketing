## The Narrative Spine

Where Tenex sells a **position in history**, Every sells a **place to stand while history accelerates**. The spine:

1. **The pace claim** — AI moves too fast for you to track alone.
2. **The vantage point** — we live at the edge ("at the absolute tip of the spear") and report back daily.
3. **The bundle promise** — one subscription covers everything you need: reviews on release day, playbooks, insights, and the tools themselves.
4. **The invitation** — join the universe; build what comes next.

It's a *membership* narrative, not a *destiny* narrative. The reader isn't warned (Tenex) or pitched a product (pm-world) — they're invited into a clubhouse at the frontier.

## Voice Attributes

- **Curious, not alarmist.** "After Automation: AI progress creates more work for humans, not less." The frame is opportunity and fascination.
- **First-person operational.** Flagship stories are confessions from their own company: "We Gave Every Employee an AI Agent. Here's What We're Doing Differently Now." "My Editor Caught Me Sounding Like AI. Now AI Catches Me First."
- **Opinionated reviewer authority.** "Vibe Check: Opus 4.8—Anthropic Should've Rounded Up to 5." "Fable 5 Is the Best Coding Model in the World." Verdicts, not summaries.
- **Literary playfulness.** Headlines pun and allude: "Socrates as a Service," "Notes From the Foothills of the Singularity," "The SaaSpocalypse," "Trekonomics."
- **Builder-direct when selling.** "Stop Planning Your AI Strategy. Start Executing It." "Work with builders, not followers."
- **Pragmatic-precise in product copy.** "An AI assistant for your email—for $15/month." "Effortless voice dictation—work 3x faster." No mysticism at the cash register.

## Copy Mechanics (with examples)

### 1. The totalizing subscription claim
> "**The Only Subscription You Need** to Stay at the Edge of AI"
One superlative, then receipts (the checklist popup: reviews on release day / playbooks / insights / apps). The claim is absolute; the proof is itemized.

### 2. Edge/frontier spatial language
The brand's home is a *place*: "stay at the **edge** of AI," "**Dispatches from the frontiers** of AI," "Notes From the **Foothills** of the Singularity," "at the absolute **tip of the spear**." Membership = access to territory.

### 3. Named beats as promises
"**Vibe Check**" (model verdicts), "**Context Window**" (what matters now), "**Putting AI to Work**," "**The New Rules of Writing**," "**What Comes Next**" (the newsletter itself). Each recurring name compresses a reader promise into two words.

### 4. We-did-it-first operational proof
> "How We Run a 25-person Company on Four AI Agents — plus sample prompts to build your own"
> "The Folder Is the Agent: I'm running 44 AI agents across multiple projects. Each one is just a model pointed at a folder."
> "This Is How the Every Editorial Team Uses AI — We're publishing our editorial guidelines"
Formula: confession + concrete number + replicable artifact (prompts, guidelines, plugin).

### 5. The qualifying disqualifier (consulting)
> "We work with organizations **ready to transform, not experiment**. If that's you, let's talk."
One sentence that filters tire-kickers and flatters the qualified.

### 6. Maker-vs-suit positioning
> "AI training, adoption, and innovation — **from makers, not management consultants**."
> "**Work with builders, not followers.**"
> "...from the team that **writes the playbooks, ships the products, and trains top teams** in AI."
The triple verb (writes/ships/trains) IS the differentiation — nobody else can claim all three.

### 7. Borrowed-scale credentials
> "$175B+ AUM our clients represent" / "16K+ GitHub stars" / "130K+ newsletter subscribers" / "Trusted by 100,000 builders"
Media metrics, code metrics, and client metrics stacked as one credibility wall.

### 8. Verb-stamped products
Read. Email. Speak. Listen. Write. Organize. Then each app gets exactly one outcome line: "Your AI writing assistant with taste—**publish with confidence**." "Automatic file organization **so you can find what you need**."

### 9. The enablement testimonial
> "rather than relying on an outside firm to make us AI native, Every **enabled our team to do it ourselves**... gave us the know-how and tools to **keep building without them**."
The anti-lock-in promise, voiced by a client. Strongest consulting line on the site.

### 10. Velocity flexes
> "Reviews of new AI models **on release day**"
> "Fable 5 Power User Camp" scheduled ~72 hours after the model dropped
> "This is insane work. We pretty much **built a whole new product in two days**."
Speed appears as fact, never as adjective.

### 11. Reframe headlines (essay titles that flip a default)
> "AI Is Ready. Organizations Aren't."
> "After Automation: AI progress creates more work for humans, not less"
> "How I Escaped AI Autopilot: The more reliable AI gets, the less we check its work."
Formula: state the consensus, reverse the subject.

## Reusable Rules

1. **Claim the territory, then staff it.** "The edge" only works because beats, vibe checks, and camps continuously prove presence. Don't claim a frontier you don't post from daily.
2. **Make the company the case study.** Your own operations, numbers, and screwups are the most differentiated content you own.
3. **Verdicts > summaries.** Reviews take a side ("Should've rounded up to 5") — that's why they're cited.
4. **Name your recurring formats** and never break their promise.
5. **One verb per product; one outcome per one-liner.**
6. **Superlative claims get itemized receipts within one viewport.**
7. **Sell enablement, not dependency** — "keep building without them" is the modern services close.
8. **Playful headlines, precise checkout copy.** Literary at the top of funnel, dollar-specific at the bottom ("$15/month").
