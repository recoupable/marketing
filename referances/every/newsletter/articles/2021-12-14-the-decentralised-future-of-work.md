# Braintrust’s Founders on How to Run a Decentralised Marketplace

*Gabe Luna Ostaseski & Adam Jackson on why the way we work is broken and how to fix it  *

*You can watch this episode on *[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l9jY-xhsVY0)* or listen to *[Means of Creation on your podcast app of choice](https://pod.link/1527481453/episode/4f2a3d97a78c17651378ae0848dae400)*.*
A recurring theme on this show has been [creator](https://every.to/c/creator-economy) and user empowerment—more specifically, how founders can [build](https://every.to/c/growth) scalable businesses that don’t just extract value from their users, but instead, allow them to directly benefit from the value they help create.
In the past, web2 [gig](https://every.to/c/contractors-&-gig-workers) economy marketplaces have had to be extractive—imposing higher take-rates and lower payouts seemed to be the only way to stay in business. Many of them still struggle to be profitable.
However, our guests for this episode are using [web3](https://every.to/c/web3) to build a radically different alternative. [Gabe Luna Ostaseski](https://twitter.com/gabelunao?ref_src=twsrc%5Egoogle%7Ctwcamp%5Eserp%7Ctwgr%5Eauthor) and [Adam Jackson](https://twitter.com/adamjacksonsf) are the founders of [Braintrust](https://every.to/c/braintrust)—a decentralised talent network like Upwork or Fiverr that is user-owned and redistributes its value back to its participants.
It’s still early, but the talent on Braintrust has already been hired by the likes of Nike, Porsche, NASA, and Goldman Sachs.
In this conversation, we talk to them about:

- Why they think our current way of building marketplaces is broken

- What led them to [founding](https://every.to/c/founders) Braintrust

- The practical challenges of running a decentralized talent network; and

- Braintrust’s vision for the future of [work](https://every.to/c/work-culture)

We hope you enjoy this conversation as much as we did!
