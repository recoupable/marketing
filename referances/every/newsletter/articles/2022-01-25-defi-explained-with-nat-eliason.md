In this first episode, we explain [DeFi](https://every.to/c/defi), or Decentralized Finance.
[Cryptocurrencies](https://every.to/c/crypto) have taken a major hit over the last few days—[Bitcoin](https://every.to/c/bitcoin) and [Ethereum](https://every.to/c/ethereum) have fallen by [almost fifty percent](https://www.cnbc.com/2022/01/24/bitcoin-ether-fall-as-130-billion-wiped-off-cryptocurrency-markets-.html) from their all-time highs. However, the proponents of DeFi still believe in its long-term potential to be the future of finance.
If we zoom out a little, this belief seems to be well-founded: as of this writing, [the value](https://defillama.com/) locked in various DeFi projects is $196B. As recently as 2019, that number was merely $600M.
DeFi proponents also go on to claim that we’re just getting started—the advantages of an open and decentralized financial system have the potential to capture trillions of dollars of value.
In this episode, we're joined by [Nat Eliason](https://twitter.com/nateliason), who helps us unpack this claim. Nat is a writer, crypto engineer, and educator who’s been at the forefront of DeFi long before it became became a trend.
He brings with him a wealth of experience in this space—he teaches a very popular course called [DeFi Orientation](https://learn.nateliason.com/p/defi-orientation) which enables people to get started in DeFi and earn money from their crypto assets. He helps crypto companies with their [tokenomics](https://every.to/c/tokenomics) and smart contract development. He also writes [Almanack](https://every.to/almanack), a newsletter about DeFi, tokenomics, yield farming, and other related topics.
Nat's experience as an educator and operator lets him simplify complex theories but also demonstrate how to apply them in practice. He was the perfect guest to bring on to explain the rapidly changing world of DeFi.
