*Everything we published this week + our new newsletter!*

#### Medium Muddles

**Casey Newton** returned to The Verge this week to dig into the story behind Wednesday's news that **Medium** was offering its entire editorial staff buyouts as an alternative to a drastic stripping of editors' responsibilities. As CEO Ev Williams [wrote](https://ev.medium.com/medium-editorial-team-update-8679bcb9fe81), the company's pivot to bundled publications wasn't paying off as planned. But as Newton reveals, the story stretches back months—years, even—before this week's announcement, and is more complicated than a straight-up effort to rethink strategy. Unfolding "in the immediate aftermath of a bruising labor battle that had seen its workers fall one vote short of forming a union," he writes, the decision was unanticipated by employees who had "were told not to think about any of" the back-and-forth decision making happening at the platform. A new chapter in the recent spate of [mass](https://www.businessinsider.com/dollar-shave-club-seeking-buyer-mel-magazine-2021-3) [layoffs](https://www.nytimes.com/2021/03/24/opinion/what-are-nfts.html) in media, Newton wonders if Medium's restructuring is another example of a "dysfunctional company built to celebrate writing only to become famous for its poor treatment of writers."

#### [The NYT Wrestles with NFTs](https://www.nytimes.com/2021/03/24/opinion/what-are-nfts.html)

[![](https://pbs.twimg.com/profile_images/1327121628485054466/q3nHrFYA_normal.jpg)
Li Jin@ljin18

SURREAL

March 24th 2021, 2:25pm EST

69 Retweets1k Likes](https://twitter.com/ljin18/status/1374804468718567426)

Maybe you remember hearing about **Li Jin**'s NFT sale of the artwork (by **Annie Zhao**) for her recent piece on the [Creator Middle Class](https://li.substack.com/p/building-the-middle-class-of-the). We [released a podcast about it](https://share.transistor.fm/s/8cb82aa6), where she spoke with her buyer, James Young, who put up 13.37 Etherium (over $20K!) for the piece. Now, **Charlie Warzel **at the ***New York Times* **has written an Op-Ed which uses Li's sale as a springboard to cover an under-addressed angle of the NFT conversation: the bond created between the buyer and seller (not to mention the artist). "While NFTs as the ultimate form of patronage makes sense," he writes, "I’m still unconvinced that they help solve the problem of the internet’s class inequality." Still, Warzel makes room for Li's point that "the path to a creator middle class...is to democratize your income streams across a variety of digital platforms." It's a great dose of a few different opinions, no matter which of them you share.
