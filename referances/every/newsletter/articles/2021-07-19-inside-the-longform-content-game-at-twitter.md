# Inside the Longform Content Game at Twitter 

*“There’s a massive appetite for content that goes beyond 280“ - Head of Longform Content Nick Sallon*

*You can watch this episode on *[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W6lrIATjkSM)* or listen to *[Means of Creation on your podcast app of choice](https://pod.link/1527481453)*.*
There’s a shift afoot at Twitter. The long-time microblogging platform has recently made a series of moves to position itself beyond just tweets, including [acquiring the newsletter company Revue](https://techcrunch.com/2021/01/26/twitter-acquires-revue/) and introducing tools to [help creators make money](https://www.theverge.com/2021/2/25/22301375/twitter-super-follows-communities-paid-followers) on the platform.
When Twitter acquired Revue, the company declared that it was making a “[better home for writers](https://blog.twitter.com/en_us/topics/company/2021/making-twitter-a-better-home-for-writers),” apparently doubling down on its mission to help people create and share content with a plugged-in audience. But are we ready for longform Twitter?
Nick Sallon, Twitter’s Head of Longform Content, thinks so. We recently talked to him about all these changes at the company. We also discussed:

- Revue’s editorial philosophy

- Twitter’s value proposition to writers

- The archetype of a Twitter-native creator

- Twitter’s longform content strategy going forward

We hope you enjoy this conversation with Nick as much as we did!
