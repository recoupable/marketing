# The Sunday Digest: Consolidation, Censorship, and Creators' New Best Friend

*Plus: Today's the last day to enter our Writing Contest!*

[![](http://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1425/optimized_922f8e13-7bad-49e1-bff2-ab5dff9f22ec_2872x1201.png)](http://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1425/optimized_922f8e13-7bad-49e1-bff2-ab5dff9f22ec_2872x1201.png)

Happy Sunday!

Ever notice product placement in sci-fi blockbusters? Not the [Reese’s](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AfAzUAxWELU) from *E.T.*—we mean the future: IBM’s eerily accurate [FaceTime setup](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWwo6JpMceg) in *2001*. The airborne [McDonald’s](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U7vvwjUq12U) drive-thru in *The Fifth Element*. *Wall–E*’s first-gen [iPod](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IJ07DcGGmMg&t=52s).

It’s easy to dismiss that as cynical advertising. But these monolithic brand presences are also hints at the business climate of imagined futures. Are the corporations we’re seeing the only ones left? What else survived? What got bought? Is everyone else in *I, Robot* obsessed with Converse, or is it just Will Smith?

Art doesn’t happen in a vacuum. So if we’re wondering where visions like these—of a select few massive companies dominating the landscape—come from, the present is a pretty good place to look. Take this week: on the heels of a mammoth [publishing merger](https://www.theatlantic.com/ideas/archive/2020/11/penguin-random-house-simon-schuster-monster-about-amazon/617209/), [Slack became part of Salesforce’s CRM monopoly](https://every.to/divinations/why-salesforce-bought-slack-21480730) and [Amazon zeroed in on podcaster Wondery](https://www.wsj.com/articles/amazon-in-talks-to-buy-podcast-maker-wondery-11606931396?mod=djemalertNEWS). As Nathan said on Wednesday’s *Talk Therapy*, “things are getting very consolidated.” So if sci-fi flicks start setting their eyes on workplace collaboration and audio storytelling platforms…we told you so.

One industry whose future is looking a little more turbulent this weekend? The movies themselves. Warner’s decision to put all of [its big-ticket 2021 films on HBO Max](https://deadline.com/2020/12/warner-bros-2021-movie-slate-hbo-max-matrix-4-dune-in-the-heights-1234649760/) the same day they debut theatrically seems like a precedent-setter. Almost every major studio now has its own streamer (or *is* one), so a large-scale shift in HBO’s direction is entirely possible. Talk about consolidation.

Actually, that’s exactly what we did this week. We talked about plenty more, too—from eCommerce and government restrictions to the weird fun-house mirror that is LinkedIn.

It’s all below—plus news, and our favorite reads of the moment. We hope you dig it.

#### Gebru’s Story Echoes Through Tech

On Wednesday, Google researcher Timnit Gebru, whose research and work on ethics and racial bias in A.I. have made her one of the field’s strongest voices, [announced she had been fired](https://twitter.com/timnitGebru/status/1334352694664957952) for speaking out on censorship and inequity at the company. [The](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/12/03/technology/google-researcher-timnit-gebru.html) [media](https://www.wired.com/story/prominent-ai-ethics-researcher-says-google-fired-her/?utm_social-type=owned&mbid=social_twitter&utm_brand=wired&utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=social) [took](https://www.platformer.news/p/the-withering-email-that-got-an-ethical) [note](https://onezero.medium.com/noted-a-i-ethicist-timnit-gebru-leaves-google-following-tense-email-exchange-f602cdb7fe89).
