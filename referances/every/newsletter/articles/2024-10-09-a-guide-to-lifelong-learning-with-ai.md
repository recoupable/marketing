# A Guide to Lifelong Learning—With AI

*Dwarkesh Patel and Simon Eskildsen on their motivation to learn, curating information, and the spaced repetition method*

[Dwarkesh Patel](https://x.com/dwarkesh_sp) and [Simon Eskildsen](https://x.com/Sirupsen) are on a mission to learn everything—and they’re using LLMs to get there faster.

Dan Shipper interviewed [the](https://youtu.be/D_4AYLr_hFI?si=1qoQ4-7QLteJT2Ob) [two](https://youtu.be/Vm49oIPtyRE?si=nPsCBpTTscjhF_N4) on our podcast [AI & I](https://every.to/podcast). Patel hosts the [Dwarkesh Podcast](https://www.dwarkeshpatel.com/), where he goes deep with guests like [Mark Zuckerberg](https://www.dwarkeshpatel.com/p/mark-zuckerberg?r=acb8b&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web), [Tony Blair](https://www.dwarkeshpatel.com/p/tony-blair?r=acb8b&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web), and [Marc Andreessen](https://www.dwarkeshpatel.com/p/marc-andreessen?r=acb8b&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web). Before each conversation, Patel learns as much as he can about his interviewee’s field of expertise. Recently, he’s integrated AI into that process.

As for Eskildsen, Dan first [interviewed](https://every.to/superorganizers/how-to-build-a-learning-machine-299655) him in 2020, when he told us about using books to go from being a Shopify intern to the company’s director of production engineering. Eskildsen’s thirst to learn continues today in his role as cofounder and CEO of AI startup [turbopuffer](https://turbopuffer.com/), where he’s building a search engine to make vector search—information retrieval that uses machine learning to gather [context](https://every.to/chain-of-thought/how-language-models-work)—easy and affordable to run at scale.

Read on for some of Patel and Eskildsen’s tips on learning better—and faster—with AI. We cover:

- What drives us to learn

- How to curate great information

- How to use AI to accelerate learning

- How to remember what you learn

- The future of learning
