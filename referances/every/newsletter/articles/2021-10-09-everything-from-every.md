### [I Was Wrong About Olympus](https://every.to/almanack/olympus-dao)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_newsprint_safak-atalay-9Z-SgyNBlhU-unsplash.jpeg)](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_newsprint_safak-atalay-9Z-SgyNBlhU-unsplash.jpeg)
*Nat Eliason/ Almanack*
Nat isn't afraid to admit when he's wrong! And this week, he takes another look at Olympus ($OHM) to find that not only is it *not *a Ponzi scheme, but it might be one of the most important DeFi protocols ever built. By combining a bonds program for liquidity and staking incentives for stability (and creating a program for other crypto project to do the same), Olympus created a cryptocurrency backed by more than just memes. As a store of value, OHM could even replace Bitcoin.
[Read.](https://every.to/almanack/olympus-dao)

### [What is “Intrinsic” Value?](https://every.to/divinations/what-is-intrinsic-value)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_Brutus_Eid_Mar.jpeg)](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_Brutus_Eid_Mar.jpeg)

*Nathan Baschez / Divinations*
Prompted by a recent statement from the CEO of JP Morgan Chase, Nathan gets philosophical on us. Rather than debate whether crypto (particularly Bitcoin) has "intrinsic value" or not, Nathan argues that the concept of "intrinsic value" is...not really a thing. In fact, we'd do better to abandon the term altogether, because "value is fundamentally non-fungible. It always connects back to individual humans with individual circumstances, knowledge, desires, and dreams."
[Read.](https://every.to/divinations/what-is-intrinsic-value)

### [Do “Stress Cancelling” Headsets Work?](https://every.to/superorganizers/cove-review-stress-cancelling-technology)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_996A5403-1095-456A-A4B8-38499707BE7E.png)](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_996A5403-1095-456A-A4B8-38499707BE7E.png)

*Dan Shipper / Superorganizers*
Wouldn't it be nice if we could turn our stress off with the touch of a button? The [Cove headset](https://www.feelcove.com/) claims to do just that, and in a way that's totally backed by science! But for $490, does it actually work? Dan puts the device to the test—and fully examines the science of stress-reduction and affective touch—in this week's Superorganizers.
[Read.](https://every.to/superorganizers/cove-review-stress-cancelling-technology)

### [The End of Software, Again](https://every.to/napkin-math/the-end-of-software-again)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_napkin_math.jpeg)](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_napkin_math.jpeg)

*Evan Armstrong / Napkin Math*
This week Evan proposes his new theory about the future of software businesses—one where niche, industry-specific software is subsidized by businesses bundling financial products. Citing examples like Shopify, Veeva, and [Toast](https://every.to/napkin-math/toast-a-vision-of-software-s-future), he walks through the implications of what this disruption could do to alter the balance of power in software.
[Read.](https://every.to/napkin-math/the-end-of-software-again)

### [A Primer on the Parasocial Economy](https://every.to/cybernaut/a-primer-on-the-parasocial-economy)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_abigail-lynn-rdmJc2Os4EM-unsplash.jpeg)](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1895/optimized_abigail-lynn-rdmJc2Os4EM-unsplash.jpeg)

*Fadeke Adegbuyi & Yash Bagal/ Cybernaut*
Back in July, Fadeke showed us how social media platforms are encouraging [parasocial relationships](https://every.to/cybernaut/the-blurred-lines-of-parasocial-relationships) and blurring the line between "fan" and "friend." This week, Fadeke and Yash prove that those parasocial relationships are key to making the creator economy work. This primer illustrates *how* platforms (and creators) are monetizing parasocial relationships—from Super Follow subscriptions on Twitter, to tips for Twitch streamers, to celebrity Cameo videos.
[Read.](https://every.to/cybernaut/a-primer-on-the-parasocial-economy)
