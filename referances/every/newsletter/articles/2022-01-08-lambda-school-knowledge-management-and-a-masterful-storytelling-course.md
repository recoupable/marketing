Bloom Institute of Technology, or the company formerly known as Lambda School, has stirred up no small amount of controversy. If you search up "Lambda School" on Twitter you'll find some of the most polarized takes on the internet. This week on Napkin Math, Evan dives headfirst into the tornado of CEO Austen Allred's haters and stans to find the truth about this coding bootcamp. ## [PARA on Paper](https://every.to/praxis/para-on-paper)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1973/optimized_newsprint_photo-1494599948593-3dafe8338d71.jpeg)](https://every.to/praxis/para-on-paper)

*Christina Luo / Praxis*
This article is for all fans of knowledge management and especially [Tiago Forte](https://twitter.com/fortelabs)'s Building a Second Brain system. After completing the [BASB](https://www.buildingasecondbrain.com/) course, Christina built upon Forte's PARA system to create her own: PARA on Paper. In this guest post, she takes readers through the detailed steps of her system and finds, "The moment I realized I didn’t need PARA—or any organizational framework for that matter—in order to take action, was the moment I began to understand it...By starting at the smallest, most actionable level, and only adding layers when the need arises, your time shifts from maintenance to making things happen."

## [How the Creator Crisis forced artists to be founders](https://constine.substack.com/p/how-the-creator-crisis-forced-artists)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1973/optimized_https___bucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com_public_images_6e6a26a3-6142-48ac-9a73-3e578662aaf8_1200x670.png)](https://constine.substack.com/p/how-the-creator-crisis-forced-artists)

*Josh Constine / PressClub*
In this interview/article, Josh Constine chats with Patreon's CEO Jack Conte about how the creator crisis spurred a startup renaissance. Although creators were originally left behind with social media platforms refusing to include monetization for them, they now have a plethora of tools to sustain their businesses. And these tools are being streamlined so artists have more time to focus on their art. According to Conte, “This is a renaissance that we are going through. The amount of funding and celebration for the arts that we're about to see at scale across the entire planet — it literally is going to make the first Renaissance seem like old news.”
[Read.](https://constine.substack.com/p/how-the-creator-crisis-forced-artists)

## [Interview with Ryan Petersen, founder and CEO of Flexport](https://noahpinion.substack.com/p/interview-ryan-petersen-ceo-of-flexport)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/1973/optimized_https___bucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com_public_images_2cb490ca-d6e8-4d91-8525-ff410e14ca62_3333x5000.jpeg)](https://noahpinion.substack.com/p/interview-ryan-petersen-ceo-of-flexport)

*Noah Smith / Noahpinion*
For the last year or so, the global economy has been hammered by a massive [supply chain crunch](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2021-12-15/supply-chain-latest-the-supply-chain-logjam-reaches-65-billion?sref=R8NfLgwS). The phenomenon is impossibly complex, encompassing a dizzying array of factors — demand shifts, the shift to e-commerce, Chinese industrial crackdowns, Covid, inflation, legacy regulations, trade imbalances, and much much more. Last October, [Ryan Petersen](https://twitter.com/typesfast), the founder and CEO of the supply chain software company Flexport, [took a trip around the port of Long Beach](https://twitter.com/typesfast/status/1451543776992845834) and tweeted about a specific outdated regulation that was holding up the flow of goods. The city swiftly [changed the regulation](https://twitter.com/RobertGarcia/status/1451679404757688320?s=20), and the congestion situation [improved](https://ktla.com/news/local-news/how-much-have-efforts-to-ease-l-a-port-congestion-helped/).
In this interview, [Noah Smith](https://noahpinion.substack.com/) talks with Ryan how the U.S. and global supply chain system fell apart, and what policy changes and technological upgrades we can use to put it back together. If you want to understand the supply chain problem, start here.
[Read.](https://noahpinion.substack.com/p/interview-ryan-petersen-ceo-of-flexport)
