## [In Praise of the Meandering Career](https://every.to/p/in-praise-of-the-meandering-career)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2729/optimized_path.png)](https://every.to/p/in-praise-of-the-meandering-career)
*Simone Stolzoff*
Success isn't always a straight-line journey. Simone Stolzoff's exploration of the 'dark horse' career trajectory challenges conventional wisdom, suggesting a non-linear path might lead to more fulfillment. Referencing a study by Harvard researchers Todd Rose and Ogi Ogas, Stolzoff discusses how unconventional professionals often experience high levels of satisfaction in their work. These 'dark horses' meander in and out of jobs or industries to find their perfect fit, drawing a unique map of their career journey rather than following a predetermined compass. This essay invites us to reconsider the traditional career path, encouraging curiosity and adaptability in an era of rapid change.

## [America's Electrical Grid Is an Incredible Feat of Engineering -And Its Biggest Bottleneck](https://every.to/p/america-s-electrical-grid-is-an-incredible-feat-of-engineering-and-it-s-falling-apart)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2729/optimized_Screen_Shot_2023-08-15_at_2.21.29_PM.png)](https://every.to/p/america-s-electrical-grid-is-an-incredible-feat-of-engineering-and-it-s-falling-apart)
*Anna- Sofia Lesiv*
America's power grid, an engineering marvel, is struggling under the strain of extreme weather events caused by climate change and desperately needs modernization, asserts Anna-Sofia Lesiv in this detailed exploration of the country's electrical infrastructure. The grid is a complex network taking power from various sources to homes and vehicles, but despite its sophistication, altering any part of it is a huge challenge. The piece delves into the state of the electric grid today and its development into a government-regulated utility from a high-competition private industry. Lesiv emphasizes that the grid is the country's greatest bottleneck in increasing energy abundance, considering the anticipated rise in electricity consumption due to the growing number of electric vehicles. She also explores the future of the grid and the urgent need to integrate renewable energy sources, despite the challenges this poses.

## [The Internet is Still Early](https://every.to/napkin-math/the-internet-is-still-early)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2729/optimized_web.png)](https://every.to/napkin-math/the-internet-is-still-early)
*Evan Armstrong / Napkin Math*
Evan Armstrong sets out to demystify the internet's role in the evolution of society. Far from being yesterday's revolution, the internet is constantly redefining the boundaries of possibilities, from altering organizational structures to changing the way we read books. Despite having around 70% global usage, the internet is still underappreciated for its transformative power that is still unfolding. In his essay, Armstrong navigates the reader through the intricate web of the internet's influence, touching on how it's not only collapsing organizational roles but also enabling ambitious missions like never before. With real-world examples from Amazon to the resurgence of books, he captures the enigmatic and paradoxical nature of the internet's impact.
