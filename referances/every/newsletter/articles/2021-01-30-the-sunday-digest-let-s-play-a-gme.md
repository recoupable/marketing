# The Sunday Digest: Let's Play a $GME

*Everything we published + a recap of the wildest week in...weeks*

Happy Sunday!
This week, the [news](https://www.axios.com/forbes-paid-newsletter-subscriptions-ab423ae1-0b90-4863-acad-40a373adce80.html) was [us](https://www.businessinsider.com/every-publication-leaves-substack-media-collective-newsletter-2021-1). We’re here now! And now you are, too, so take a look around: we’ve got a new name, a new platform with a custom typeface, and some sweet new publication logos (food for all the Serif Stans out there). Plus, [a founder’s letter](http://every.to/letter) from Dan and Nathan, explaining the reasoning behind our move, and why it goes deeper than just a rebrand.
Being the News was fun. It also lasted only a few hours. For us, January 26th may always be Launch Day but for many others, it’ll be Stonk Day. Or the Day Melvin Stood Still. Or…something. You’ve probably got a better name; please do leave any in the comments. GameStop’s meteoric rise may have been in the works [for months](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2021-01-28/why-are-reddit-wall-street-bets-traders-buying-gamestop-and-fighting-hedge-funds), but its break into the mainstream has dominated the final week of this month.
Takes are flying fast and thick: this is a righteous [uprising](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2021-01-27/gamestop-short-squeeze-is-rage-against-the-financial-machine); it can [only end badly](https://www.cnbc.com/2021/01/28/leon-cooperman-on-gamestop-reddit-speculators-im-not-damning-them-but-it-will-end-in-tears.html); it has everything to do with [not enough sex](https://twitter.com/profgalloway/status/1354532507723640835) (?). And while we can’t ignore the hits of pure dopamine we get from watching hedge fund managers sweat on their CNBC Zoom feeds, characterizing this as a full-on rebellion feels maybe overstated. For one: stocks cost money. For another: though we don't doubt the sincerity of the movement, encouraging online day traders to pile on feels like an easy way for certain [people](https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1354174279894642703) whose pockets need no extra padding to grab some easy cred—or[make even more money](https://www.vice.com/en/article/y3gepx/investment-firms-are-the-big-winners-of-the-gamestop-stock-revolution-so-far). (Some actual GameStop workers, it’s worth noting, [aren’t particularly jazzed](https://www.inputmag.com/culture/gamestop-employees-on-r-wallstreetbets-theyre-rubbing-it-in-our-faces) about their employer’s sudden jump in popularity.)
None of that is to say this phenomenon should be ignored as a call for more level ground in the market. But it’s worth, dare we say it, thinking through. It will be interesting to see where things end up when the dust settles.
But let’s put a stop to the GameStop takes and get back to us. That’s what this space is all about, anyway—and there’s plenty to round up in this inaugural week of the Every Sunday Digest: A look at the run-up and road forward for this here site from its founders; a passion economy palate cleanser; and your first update from The Prediction Game, at the end of an absurdly eventful month that still managed to be predictable to some. The point being: new home, same digest.
Until we have more to say...

### [Finding the Truth](https://every.to/superorganizers/finding-the-truth)

by **Dan Shipper **in ***Superorganizers***
We should all be reading as much as Dan is—not least because it might help us write about our literary intake as well as he does. Here, he starts by identifying a problem with the books he's been reading: they seem to contradict each other without acknowledging it. That's fine enough, but when you're dealing with nonfiction books that recommend new and different strategies for rewiring your brain, how do you know what to try? Or how long to do it for? These questions don't have simple answers—and maybe we shouldn't tie ourselves in knots looking for them. Or, as Dan writes, "So what if the edges of our brains are messy?"
[Read](https://every.to/superorganizers/finding-the-truth) 🔒 (*9 minutes*)

### [A Local News Revival](https://every.to/napkin-math/a-local-news-revival)

by **Adam Keesling** in ***Napkin Math***
In a time where [everyone](https://blog.twitter.com/en_us/topics/company/2021/making-twitter-a-better-home-for-writers.html) and their [cousin](https://www.theverge.com/2021/1/29/22255876/facebook-newsletter-tools-independent-journalists-writers-substack-revue) is cashing in on the newsletter boom, here is a more granular look at one of the phenomenon's more intriguing use cases: local newsletters. That's right, news*letters, *not *papers*—in fact, that's a crucial part of this essay's meticulous construction. Reaching back to understand "why newspapers died," Adam lays out the qualities that newsletters might be able to excavate and ultimately improve on. This being *Napkin Math*, the argument is the opposite of Pie in the Sky; by the time you reach the paragraphs with as many numbers as letters, you'll know you're reading a fascinating piece of business theory bolstered by clear, strong research.
[Read](https://every.to/napkin-math/a-local-news-revival) 🔒 (*11 minutes*)

There are so many knots in the web of incidents that are making up the current state of the market, or that have happened because of it: The ex-CEO of a pet food startup buying [nine million](https://markets.businessinsider.com/news/stocks/gamestop-stock-price-investment-ryan-cohen-chewy-founder-1700-return-2021-1-1030000866#:~:text=Cohen%20amassed%20a%209%20million,the%20long%2Dgame%20on%20GameStop.) shares of Gamestop stock, chiding the company for its "[UNWILLINGNESS TO RAPIDLY EMBRACE THE DIGITAL ECONOMY](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1326380/000101359420000821/rc13da3-111620.pdf)" and receiving three board seats as a result before seeing his long position pay off to a near-mythic degree. There's **Chamath Palihapitiya **putting [things in perspective](https://twitter.com/therecount/status/1354495573706080257) on TV, and **AOC**'s Twitch forum with...TheStockGuy?
That's been part of the wonder of this week: the influx of blissfully unpredictable (or maybe inevitable, actually) alliances, direct or indirect.
