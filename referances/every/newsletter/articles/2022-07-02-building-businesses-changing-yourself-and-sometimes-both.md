## [Changing the world to change yourself](https://every.to/superorganizers/changing-the-world-to-change-yourself)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2209/optimized_superorganizers.jpg)](https://every.to/superorganizers/changing-the-world-to-change-yourself)
*Dan Shipper / Superorganizers*
Dan has a theory: most people who are trying to change the world—that is, build world-changing companies—are actually trying to change themselves. Either they’re looking for respect, or they’re looking for love and connection. Sometimes, it’s a combination of both.
Why does Dan think this? Well, because it’s been true for him. In this post, Dan unpacks how his startup journey intertwines with his quest for respect, love, and connection—and where he stands on that quest now.
[Read.](https://every.to/superorganizers/changing-the-world-to-change-yourself)

## [Oh Shit, You’re Going Through a Reorg](https://every.to/rethinking-resilience/so-you-re-going-through-a-reorg)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2209/optimized_reorg.jpg)](https://every.to/rethinking-resilience/so-you-re-going-through-a-reorg)
*Jason Shen / Rethinking Resilience*
The word “reorg” can strike fear into even the most seasoned professional and leave ambitious achievers wondering: Was all my hard work for nothing? Do I have to start over? What does this mean for my career? There are no easy answers to these questions, but one thing’s for certain: the change and uncertainty of a reorg calls for a resilient mindset.
In this second edition of our ongoing series Rethinking Resilience, Jason applies his [resilience framework](https://every.to/rethinking-resilience/so-you-re-going-through-a-reorg)* *to the dreaded reorg and shows how, by thinking in terms of responding, restoring, rebuilding, and reflecting, you can navigate a reorg with courage, acumen, and empathy.
[Read.](https://every.to/rethinking-resilience/so-you-re-going-through-a-reorg)
