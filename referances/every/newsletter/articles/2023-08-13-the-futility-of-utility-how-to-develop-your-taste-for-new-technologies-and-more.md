## [How to Develop Your Taste for New Technologies](https://every.to/chain-of-thought/how-to-develop-your-taste-for-new-technologies)

*Dan Shipper / Chain of Thought*
Is it performance art or a bot? That's the question with which Dan Shipper grapples in this piece about GrimesAI, an AI bot that imitates the style and persona of musician and artist Grimes with such uncanny accuracy it might just be a technical marvel. Dan delves into the bot's enigmatic world, exploring its strikingly human-like tweets, chilling humor, and the unique techno-futuristic vibe it seems to embody. In a world increasingly shaped by AI, the line between human and machine is blurring—and while this might be unsettling for some, he encourages us to see this as an opportunity to develop our taste for new technologies in order to anticipate and capitalize on the major cultural and social shifts they often drive.

## [The Futility of Utility](https://every.to/napkin-math/the-futility-of-utility)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2724/optimized_scale.png)](https://every.to/napkin-math/the-futility-of-utility)
*Evan Armstrong / Napkin Math*
Struggling with the rationality, or lack thereof, of personal choices, Evan Armstrong revisits and revamps a previous essay for his latest piece on utility. Drawing from his past attempts to create a formula to maximize the value of his limited funds, he finds the results inconsistent and unreliable. He questions why people (including himself) often make self-destructive choices despite knowing better, and investigates it both at an individual and organizational level. He discusses his observations of utility maximization failures at various levels, from his work as a missionary to his experiences in the corporate world. Evan's journey to find a universal truth to explain these inconsistencies leads him to the realization that perhaps the perfect choice is not as important as the one that gets us close enough.
[Read](https://every.to/napkin-math/the-futility-of-utility)

## [How to Pay Off Your Emotional Debt](https://every.to/p/how-to-pay-off-your-emotional-debt)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2724/optimized_ujx0lBhTzbSnI6XvdtEcM5REcGj4c--9qf3rBg0kvYBZvY5lKI-o2HU6FfOAvVIAh352BaTpGOAfQL8HaBaszjD4AAyH3HNGzC3li5lKpjTr_dz4fUpuVWX9iIgX.png)](https://every.to/p/how-to-pay-off-your-emotional-debt)
*Jonny Miller*
Struggling with emotional burnout and accumulated stress? Dive into Jonny Miller's exploration of "emotional debt," which he compares to the concept of technical debt in fast-growing startups. Much like startups that might hurriedly assemble code that needs future refining, humans often repress emotions during stressful situations, leading to the accumulation of emotional debt that, if not addressed, can result in emotional burnout. Jonny, who has guided hundreds through their emotional journeys, unravels how to effectively release this debt, emphasizing the importance of physically experiencing emotions and adopting an approach called "somatic surfing."
