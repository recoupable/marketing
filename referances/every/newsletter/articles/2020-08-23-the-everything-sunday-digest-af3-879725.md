#### ⚡️ Reading suggestions from Superorganizers

**[Fire and Motion](https://www.joelonsoftware.com/2002/01/06/fire-and-motion/)**, by Joel Spolsky *(1,499 words)*

Fire and Motion struck me because it’s old but its message is still fresh. It’s an article by Joel Spolsky, creator of StackOverflow, Trello, and more talking about having days where he’s tired and demotivated and has trouble getting himself to work. I’ve written about the same thing in [Productivity Cycles](https://every.to/superorganizers/productivity-cycles-294288) — but it’s always reassuring to hear about it from someone I respect as much as Joel. — *Dan*

#### 🔮 Reading suggestions from Divinations

**[NYT CEO Mark Thompson on subscriptions, platforms and internal Slack fights](https://overcast.fm/+QL2ej7GFI)** 🎧, by Peter Kafka in the *Recode Media* podcast *(57 min)*

“Great podcast with the outgoing NYT CEO who presided over their subscription strategy for the past 8 years. Two really tangible take-aways: 1) they don’t care so much about individual viral articles as they care about creating direct, habitual readership, 2) long discounted trial periods work well to move people from ‘interested’ to ‘loyal’.”
