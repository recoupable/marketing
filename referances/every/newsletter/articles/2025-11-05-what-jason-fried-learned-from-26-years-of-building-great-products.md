Running a business is not that interesting to **[Jason Fried](https://x.com/jasonfried)**—even though his bootstrapped company earns tens of millions in profit every year, and he’s been at it for over two decades.

What lights him up—and has him waxing lyrical about architecture, watches, and cars—is making products, particularly the kind that are built around a single, coherent idea. The kind that leaves people with a near-spiritual sense of satisfaction.

Fried is the cofounder and CEO of [37signals](https://37signals.com/), the makers of product management tool [Basecamp](https://basecamp.com/) and web application framework [Ruby on Rails](https://rubyonrails.org/), and you can see his obsession with product reflected back by his loyal users. In this episode of *AI & I, ***Dan Shipper **and Fried talk about everything he’s learned about building something whole, from the way he designs software to his philosophy around running a company.
