## [How to Do Hard Things](https://every.to/superorganizers/how-to-do-hard-things)

![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/post/cover/2143/david-gavi-Ijx8OxvKrgM-unsplash_2.png)

*Casey Rosengren / Superorganizers*
It’s an exciting week for Superorganizers, because we’re finally covering a topic that is near and dear to Dan’s heart: Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT). ACT is a psychological framework designed to help work through the difficult thoughts and feelings that come up when you’re doing meaningful work. (You know, like starting a company?) ACT has made a big impact on Dan’s life, so this week, he invited fellow founder and ACT aficionado Casey Rosengren to walk us through the essentials: what ACT is, how it works, and six core processes the framework teaches for debugging your brain when it gets stuck.
