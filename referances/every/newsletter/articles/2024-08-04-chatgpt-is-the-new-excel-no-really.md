## Data mining

**MetaAI’s unexpected power users.** If you had to guess where in the world Meta was finding the highest utilization of its Meta AI product—an AI assistant—where would it be? If you guessed “India,” you’d be correct. During the company’s second-quarter earnings call, its [chief financial officer said](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/meta-says-india-largest-market-031244665.html), “People have used Meta AI for billions of queries since we first introduced it. We're seeing particularly promising signs on WhatsApp in terms of retention and engagement, which has coincided with **India becoming our largest market for Meta AI usage**.” [Emphasis added] India is already Meta’s largest install base by far, and it’s not even close:

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/3177/optimized_AD_4nXfVS2AMXB9bhmDScsUbDUFkTdRLCG0MZ0nF45Kahn-dxNva8dbnLRrC8s4gWcecpujTJniiMC22qQAkzolUBQC0kQrv5IdeCDH1MxOwCkxBK4abw1nFxqFq.png)](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/3177/optimized_AD_4nXfVS2AMXB9bhmDScsUbDUFkTdRLCG0MZ0nF45Kahn-dxNva8dbnLRrC8s4gWcecpujTJniiMC22qQAkzolUBQC0kQrv5IdeCDH1MxOwCkxBK4abw1nFxqFq.png)
*Source: *[Statista](https://www.statista.com/statistics/268136/top-15-countries-based-on-number-of-facebook-users).
India has almost twice as many Facebook users as the United States, driven mostly by WhatsApp—which also happens to be the primary delivery mechanism for Meta AI. Not only is WhatsApp powering India’s (extremely rapid) [on-ramp to the internet](https://www.therandomwalk.co/p/social-media-is-only-getting-bigger?open=false#%C2%A7social-media-is-the-internet-in-some-parts-of-the-world-and-its-just-getting-started), the country is one of the few large developing nations expected to grow over the next few decades. If China was the last quarter-century’s “new market to conquer,” India is very likely to be the next, and Meta’s substantial foothold ought to be the envy of its peers. To be fair, the quarterly revenue of $389 million from WhatsApp Business (its enterprise platform) and Payments is a rounding error for a company generating $38 billion in advertising, but the company did go out of its way to note [73 percent year-over-year growth](https://www.therandomwalk.co/p/meta-in-india?open=false#%C2%A7whatsapp-business-the-king-of-other). Even if that growth slows to 50 percent, it won’t be long before Meta’s beachhead in India becomes the crown jewel of its growth story—and its AI growth story as well.—[Moses Sternstein](https://www.therandomwalk.co/)

## Alignment

Your new BFF. There's a new friend in town, and it's called...[friend](https://www.friend.com/)? It's a digital AI companion that you wear on your chest like a pendant and speak to like an old pal. Just wait until it starts giving you dating advice—suddenly you're living in a *Black Mirror* episode titled "Swipe Left for Skynet." In all seriousness, it does have potential. Loneliness is an [epidemic](https://www.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/surgeon-general-social-connection-advisory.pdf), and a reason why is the erosion of social skills. Millions of people are desperate for connection but lacking the tools to make it happen. That's where AI friends could genuinely make a difference: an always-available, judgment-free conversation partner, like a personal trainer for your social development. You can practice small talk, work on your conversational timing, and maybe even rehearse those painful social scenarios—all without the fear of real-world consequences. We might just be on the brink of a social revolution, one where AI doesn't replace human connection, but instead becomes the training wheels that help us rediscover it.—[Ashwin Sharma](https://x.com/Ashwinreads)

## Sentiment analysis

*Dan’s piece about the *[great AI unbundling](https://every.to/chain-of-thought/the-great-ai-unbundling)* drew a contrary view:*

> “I liked this post a lot, and especially the introduction about JTBD [job to be done]. But I don’t think it’s true. Software startups have a moat partly because building a team of designers and engineers to create and maintain a product is hard. But increasingly, you don’t need to do that anymore; AI can write code and build products, too. This democratization means that everyone gets to be a SaaS founder. But rather than paying by subscription for a fixed set of polished features, the ‘products’ will be one-off, single-use, disposable software that’s good enough—and free. ‘It’s different this time’ because foundation models are very different from the stuff of which VisiCalc was made. (Probably I’m both wrong and right about this: The niches in which SaaS work will get tougher, but they’ll still be around.)”—*An AI startup founder*
