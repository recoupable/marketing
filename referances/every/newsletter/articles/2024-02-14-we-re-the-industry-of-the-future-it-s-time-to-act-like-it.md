# Autonomous Vehicles Have a Problem of Narrative

Let’s ignore that this is, like, a crime. And let’s also ignore that destroying cars is a proud American tradition—one practiced by my fellow Bostonians, whether our teams [win](https://metro.co.uk/2018/02/05/here-are-five-american-cities-which-rioted-after-sporting-success-and-defeat-7289493/) or [lose](https://www.sportscasting.com/phillys-not-alone-sports-fans-cities-also-went-riot-mode/).

What I would like to call attention to is the misunderstanding that this event begat. Some in tech struggled to [empathize](https://x.com/AravSrinivas/status/1756782992859787505?s=20) with why people were upset. In the opposing corner, [on](https://x.com/JeffSpeckFAICP/status/1756807815358726194?s=20) [Twitter](https://x.com/seisinenfeoffin/status/1756743656009306485?s=20) and in private conversations with me, others expressed a gleeful sentiment about the car’s destruction, with some people unable to grok the humanity-advancing technology these cars represent.

This single incident of arson is a broader signal for what has gone wrong with how the technology industry manages its public perception in America.

During the boom years of the [2010s](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/how-americans-confidence-in-technology-firms-has-dropped-evidence-from-the-second-wave-of-the-american-institutional-confidence-poll/), when Facebook was uniting the world during events like the [Arab Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Spring) and people were generally optimistic that the new internet companies would make the world better, [polling](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/how-americans-confidence-in-technology-firms-has-dropped-evidence-from-the-second-wave-of-the-american-institutional-confidence-poll/) indicated that the average American loved tech. People had more confidence in Amazon than the press, college, non-profits, the FBI, or organized religion. The popular media embraced nearly everything we did under a halo of goodwill and good faith.

Starting with Donald Trump’s election and shifting dramatically during the pandemic, the general public started to turn against the tech industry.
