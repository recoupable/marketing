# Vibe Check: Opus 4.8—Anthropic Should’ve Rounded Up to 5

*Opus 4.8 tops both our Senior Engineer benchmark and our writing tests. It’s the most complete model we’ve tested. We just wish it had an app to match.*

Anthropic is so back.

They&rsquo;ve had the wind at their backs for the past year, riding the Claude Code wave into the rest of knowledge work. But [Opus 4.7](https://every.to/vibe-check/opus-4-7) was a hard-to-use, hard-to-love model, and the [Codex desktop](https://every.to/vibe-check/vibe-check-openai-s-codex-app-gains-ground-on-claude-code) app is clean, fast, and feels like the future. I switched to Codex full time, and even Every&rsquo;s most devoted Claude users like [Kieran Klaassen](https://every.to/@kieranklaassen) and [Katie Parrott](https://every.to/@katie.parrott12) found themselves reaching for GPT models in a way they hadn&rsquo;t in at least a year.

But Opus 4.8 is a legitimately great model, jumping to the top of the pack in the rankings (and our hearts). It bests [GPT-5.5](https://every.to/vibe-check/gpt-5-5) on our Senior Engineer benchmark by a hair (63/100 to 62/100), and it&rsquo;s the best model we&rsquo;ve tested for writing and knowledge work. It produced the best one-shot PowerPoint presentation we&rsquo;ve seen on our enterprise consulting benchmark: a crafted, well-designed deck that effectively told a story, something most models still can&rsquo;t do.

It&rsquo;s very hard to make a model that is both an incredible software engineer and a near-human writer with depth and emotional intelligence&mdash;but that&rsquo;s what this model feels like to us.

They could have called this Opus 5 and none of us would have blinked.

There are two catches. The first is that output quality is heavily dependent on effort level. Opus 4.8 at extra-high is a competitive senior engineer, while at high it&rsquo;s an adequate one. Opus 4.8 at high delivers mostly clean, expressive prose, while Opus 4.8 at medium succumbs to AI&rsquo;s worst writing tendencies.

Second, the model is better than the app around it. Opus 4.8 is strong enough to make us want to move back to Claude. But the Claude app is a mess. It has three different tabs&mdash;Chat, Code, [Cowork](https://every.to/vibe-check/vibe-check-claude-cowork-is-claude-code-for-the-rest-of-us)&mdash;that bear the scars of the harness&rsquo;s progression through time and Anthropic&rsquo;s org chart. That makes the experience feel slow and messy, and it doesn&rsquo;t allow us to easily get the most out of the model.

We get into all of this below.

## What&rsquo;s new

Anthropic is positioning Opus 4.8 as a stronger model for complex work, especially coding, agentic tasks, and long-running reasoning.

### Best on senior-engineer coding, at the right effort

At extra-high, Opus 4.8 scored 63 on our Senior Engineer Benchmark, a hair past GPT-5.5&rsquo;s 62 and a 30-point jump over Opus 4.7. At high, it falls to 42.

### The best writing model we&rsquo;ve tested

Opus 4.8 at high effort scored 79.6 on our writing benchmark, ahead of [Sonnet 4.6](https://every.to/vibe-check/vibe-check-anthropic-just-made-opus-cheaper-without-calling-it-that) (74.5), [GPT-5.5](https://every.to/vibe-check/gpt-5-5) (73), and [Opus 4.7](https://every.to/vibe-check/opus-4-7) (63). It also left fewer AI tells than any model apart from Sonnet.

### Strong everyday knowledge work, with one caveat

Faster than 4.7, better at explaining itself than GPT-5.5, and unusually good at adopting your voice from a style guide. But it hangs back and waits for instructions where GPT-5.5 runs ahead.

### A 1 million-token context window

Big enough to hold an entire codebase, a book-length manuscript, or weeks of meeting notes in a single session, and it carries context across that span better than 4.7 did.

### The model is ahead of the app

Opus 4.8 is good enough to pull us back to Claude, but the Chat/Code/Cowork split keeps Codex as the better daily harness.
