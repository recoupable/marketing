## Chain of links

**LLMs solve a really hard math problem.** Are language models glorified summarization machines, or can they actually advance our understanding of the world? This is a fundamental question in AI. This week it was revealed that they *can* make discoveries—at least in some domains. A DeepMind team used a language model [to find novel solutions](https://deepmind.google/discover/blog/funsearch-making-new-discoveries-in-mathematical-sciences-using-large-language-models/) to a longstanding open problem in mathematics. We’ll see this kind of headline more frequently.
**Progress in AI-not-kill-everyone-ism.** When GPT-4 finished training, it underwent an extensive fine-tuning process that used human supervisors to help it learn how to be helpful and harmless. It worked because humans are smarter than language models. But what happens when AI becomes smarter than humans? Will supervision work then? OpenAI released a [research paper saying that it could](https://openai.com/research/weak-to-strong-generalization)—which is significant progress.
**The future of creative interfaces.** Krea, an AI-first design tool, [launched in open beta](https://twitter.com/krea_ai/status/1734866368489722035). It uses a combination UI of text-to-image and image-to-image tools that allows for extraordinary control in AI art creation. Expect this type of UI to become commonplace in creative tools. (Note: pair this with our [recent podcast episode](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OeKEXnNP2yA) with Linus Lee for more on creative tools for thought in the AI world.) *—*[Dan Shipper](https://twitter.com/danshipper)

## The napkin math

This technology is objectively safer, but GM is kowtowing to regulators over one mistake. I can’t believe I’m about to type this, but you need an Elon-level asshole to run this kind of company, not a Detroit-based pushover. Put middle fingers up to the regulators and show that the tech works.
