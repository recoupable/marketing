#### Spiral’s going agentic. Here’s what that means.

The first version of **[Spiral](http://spiral.computer)**, our content generation tool, was a one-shot wonder: You’d feed it a prompt, and it’d spit out a content draft—say, some ideas for a tweet or headline. But if you wanted to tweak it (“Keep this, but make it more playful”), Spiral couldn’t follow your intent, and you'd end up re-prompting from scratch..
The new Spiral is more like having a ghostwriter in your corner. It maps out what you're trying to say, builds a plan, and revises alongside you. Not *quite* sure what you’re trying to say yet? It’ll interview you to help figure it out. Under the hood, it’s inspired by Claude Code. Just like its coding friend, the new Spiral makes to-do lists, reasons step by step, pulls inspiration from high-performing content, and applies proven writing principles we’ve collected.
As a result, Spiral doesn't just write *for* you. It thinks and writes *with* you. Want in on the beta? Reach out to **Danny Aziz**, Spiral’s general manager, at [[email protected]](https://every.to/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#e286838c8c9ba2879487909bcc968d).

Out with the old, more room for the new. **[Sparkle](http://makeitsparkle.co)**, our file management tool, just released a Duplicate Finder to beta users. It tracks down duplicate files by name *and* content, clearing out that endless trail of File (1), File (2), File (3), … File_Final, File_FINAL_ACTUALLY—you know the ones (or maybe you don’t… which is exactly why you should try Duplicate Finder).
