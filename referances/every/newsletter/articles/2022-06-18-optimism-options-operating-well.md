## [Selective Optimism](https://every.to/napkin-math/selective-optimism)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2192/optimized_optimism.jpeg)](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2192/optimized_optimism.jpeg)
*Evan Armstrong / Napkin Math*
Optimism is the native state of tech, and never is that tendency more apparent than when things start looking less than great. As the markets have headed south and layoffs have begun, there’s been a surge of voices calling for optimism. “It’s time to build,” etc.
As Evan argues in this week’s edition of Napkin Math, that’s all very well—to a point. But spend too much time looking through your rose-colored glasses, and you risk missing the fire that’s burning right in front of you. In this post, Evan takes a hard look at optimism in finance, specifically: when it makes sense, when it makes less sense, and what we should all make of this moment in particular.
[Read.](https://every.to/napkin-math/selective-optimism)

## [What Should You Do With Your Options During a Downturn?](https://every.to/p/what-should-you-do-with-your-options-during-a-downturn)

[![](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2192/optimized_options.jpg)](https://d24ovhgu8s7341.cloudfront.net/uploads/editor/posts/2192/optimized_options.jpg)
*Adam Keesling / Every*
Startup options aren’t the easiest thing to understand even under the best of circumstances. And this right here, what we’re experiencing right now? Not the best of circumstances. As a startup employee, you’d be forgiven for feeling a bit like that [meme of Chris Pratt](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/afraid-to-ask-andy): “I don’t know how options work, and at this point I’m afraid to ask.”
Fortunately, Adam Keesling is here to demystify this admittedly mystifying topic. In this post, Adam walks us through the decision calculus behind exercising options—particularly when market conditions are less than ideal. Among the things he encourages you to consider: how’s your liquidity? How do you feel about your company’s prospects long-term? And what’s your opportunity cost?
[Read.](https://every.to/p/what-should-you-do-with-your-options-during-a-downturn)

## [The Screaming Fangirls Who Forged the Internet](https://every.to/cybernaut/the-screaming-fangirls-who-forged-the-internet)

As founders and builders, it’s tempting to want to believe that we *make *the internet—that we hold the keys to its destiny and what it will become. But if we’re honest, we all know who truly  makes the internet what it is: the users.
This week on Cybernaut, Fadeke reviews the new book *Everything I Need I Get From You. *On the surface, it’s a book about One Direction fandom. But on a deeper level, it’s about the ways that fandoms—many of them largely female—shape the internet we all inhabit every day.
