## Fine tuning

[OpenAI and Anduril teamed up](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/openai-anduril-to-develop-anti-drone-ai-for-u-s-defense)** on anti-drone military systems**, the latest in a series of advancements in AI and defense tech. Last month, Meta [gave access to Llama](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/11/04/technology/meta-ai-military.html), its open-source AI model, to federal agencies and defense contractors (previously, its acceptable use policies prohibited military applications). Anthropic, Palantir, and AWS also announced [a partnership](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2024/11/08/anthropic-meta-pentagon-military-openai/) to build secure AI for U.S. defense intelligence.
**Meta is planning to build a **[$10 billion, 25,000-mile underwater cable](https://techcrunch.com/2024/11/29/meta-plans-to-build-a-10b-subsea-cable-spanning-the-world-sources-say/), which will be the company’s first fully owned fiber-optic network—Google is the sole owner of several global cable routes. Meta accounts for 22 percent of mobile usage worldwide; operating a private network reduces reliance on traditional telecoms and [geopolitical bottlenecks](https://nypost.com/2024/11/30/tech/meta-plans-to-build-10b-globe-spanning-undersea-internet-cable-report/).
**A single **[Bitcoin rose to $100,000 for the first time](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/12/04/technology/bitcoin-price-record.html), as President-elect Donald Trump’s pro-crypto stance and Wall Street’s bitcoin ETFs (which enable day traders to hold crypto like they would stocks) drive institutional and consumer adoption of the asset. The milestone comes just two years after FTX's collapse sent Bitcoin below $17,000.
[Trump appointed venture capitalist David Sacks](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-12-06/trump-names-david-sacks-as-white-house-ai-and-crypto-czar?accessToken=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJzb3VyY2UiOiJTdWJzY3JpYmVyR2lmdGVkQXJ0aWNsZSIsImlhdCI6MTczMzQ0NzI5OSwiZXhwIjoxNzM0MDUyMDk5LCJhcnRpY2xlSWQiOiJTTzFSU0tEV1gyUFMwMCIsImJjb25uZWN0SWQiOiI3QTM1RDE0MEZDNzU0MzVBOTlFNzRDNkVFMzg2NUYyNCJ9.xpDpvxa-hMm8_1O2UECns2Kp5l3Rd-nVlfYtZT6QD2M)** as “White House AI & Crypto Czar,”** a new role he’s creating to steward U.S. dominance across both industries. Sacks, also a part of Silicon Valley’s PayPal mafia, was a chief supporter of Trump’s campaign. In his additional role as a “special government employee,” he’ll ostensibly work closely with Elon Musk on Trump’s market deregulation measures.
**The European Union **[ordered TikTok to preserve data](https://apnews.com/article/romania-tiktok-election-european-union-7aec53655b26d1d037aea8a4add47ccb)** related to Romania’s election** after Romanian intelligence exposed a Moscow-backed operation that propelled an unknown pro-Russia candidate to front-runner status. [TikTok also lost its bid](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2ldnq5095xo) to overturn its sell-or-ban law, which requires the app to either be sold to a U.S. company or banned. Its next move is a Supreme Court appeal.
**Japan budgeted an **[additional $9.9 billion toward chip manufacturing](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-11-29/japan-earmarks-extra-9-9-billion-for-chips-and-ai-this-year) as part of the country’s $67 billion commitment to chips and AI development through 2030. As China and the U.S. lead a global race in semiconductor investment, Japan—already home to major chip materials and equipment makers—is moving to secure domestic market share.
**Eighty telecommunication companies and internet service providers** worldwide have been affected by [China’s ongoing cyberattack](https://www.politico.com/news/2024/12/03/chinese-hack-global-telecom-ongoing-00192410), including Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile. Intelligence officials say that Salt Typhoon, a hacker group affiliated with the Chinese government, targeted specific companies to access key government and corporate targets across dozens of countries.
[OpenAI kicked off its “12 days of Shipmas”](https://openai.com/12-days/) with the official launch of its previously-in-research-mode [o1 model](https://every.to/chain-of-thought/openai-s-o1-model-explained), as well as ChatGPT Pro—a $200 monthly subscription tier with access to o1, GPT-4o, and [Advanced Voice Mode](https://every.to/chain-of-thought/review-chatgpt-s-new-advanced-voice-mode). Day 2 was focused on developers: Users can fine-tune o1 on their own datasets through reinforcement fine-tuning.
[The Browser Company teased Dia, its new web browser](https://www.theverge.com/2024/12/2/24310944/dia-ai-browser-video-arc-the-browser-company) focused on integrating AI tools for everyday use. In October, TBC announced that it was pivoting away from Arc, its beloved indie browser and flagship app, to much dissent from users. Dia is meant to be a pivot into mainstream adoption and casual consumers.
[ChatGPT can now say "David Mayer."](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2024/dec/03/chatgpts-refusal-to-acknowledge-david-mayer-down-to-glitch-says-openai)** **Last weekend, a content filter error caused the chatbot to crash whenever users asked it to include the name, sparking wild theories that it was blocking references to either David Mayer de Rothschild—the British adventurer and environmentalist who is heir to the banking family’s fortune–or a professor whose name matched a security watchlist.
[Perplexity expanded its publishing platform](https://techcrunch.com/2024/12/05/perplexity-expands-its-publisher-program/)** **to include the *Los Angeles Times, Adweek*, and a dozen other news outlets, offering revenue sharing and performance metrics for publishers who let the AI startup scrape and summarize their content. The deals come as the *New York Times* and Dow Jones sue Perplexity for "content kleptocracy," alleging that the company profits from unauthorized use of their paywalled articles.
[Threads officially entered the fediverse,](https://www.fastcompany.com/91240700/threads-is-going-all-in-on-the-fediverse-and-users-have-some-questions) a decentralized network of social platforms across which users can interact, rolling out limited access to networks like Mastodon and WordPress. The fully decentralized network Bluesky recently hit 24 million users, but whether Threads’s move toward decentralization helps or hurts adoption is yet to be seen. [Mark Zuckerberg’s announcement](https://www.threads.net/@zuck/post/DDKf6uTPWSg?xmt=AQGzH_KYvkU3Q-rcEm1AkiaGtQBYblDR1xLXpXp9R15yNA) was met with mixed signals and apprehension from users.—*AV*

## Alignment

**Blame the ROI. **According to a recent [Atlantic piece](https://www.theatlantic.com/newsletters/archive/2024/11/books-briefing-gen-z-reading-books-waste-time/680586/), 82 percent of today's students prioritize financial success over intellectual exploration—a complete reversal from 1971, when most saw college as a place to wrestle with life's big questions through literature and philosophy. But the same generation that supposedly "can't focus" will spend hours mastering Python or analyzing market trends online. They haven't lost the ability to read—they're just terrified of wasting time on anything without immediate, measurable returns. But the most radical act you can take is picking up a book. Not for productivity or for career growth, but because something about string theory or ancient myths or the birth of jazz sets your mind on fire. The most valuable things we learn are the ones we didn't plan to find. That’s not waste—that’s wisdom.—[Ashwin Sharma](https://x.com/Ashwinreads)
