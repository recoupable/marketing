# All Things Technology, Film, and Media, with Eugene Wei

*And understanding how psychology affects consumer behavior*

For our guest this week on Means of Creation we are welcoming [Eugene Wei](https://twitter.com/eugenewei)!
Eugene is a former product executive who is one of the best technology bloggers online today. Since 2001, Eugene has been publishing a blog and newsletter called [Remains of the Day](https://www.eugenewei.com) where he writes about technology and media. The blog distills complicated consumer tech trends not just from a product design standpoint, but also from a user psychology perspective. His essays on [Status as a Service (StaaS](https://www.eugenewei.com/blog/2019/2/19/status-as-a-service)) and [Seeing Like an Algorithm](https://www.eugenewei.com/blog/2020/9/18/seeing-like-an-algorithm) serve as guiding mental models for the tech community.
Eugene’s past experience gives him a unique historical perspective on the development of consumer technology platforms. Most recently, Eugene was Head of Video at Oculus and before that, he led product teams at Flipboard, Erly, Hulu and Amazon.
In this interview, we talked to Eugene about:

- How he first got **interested in TikTok**

- What **other domains** could become TikTok-ified

- How his experience in **film school impacts his work as a product leader**

- How our chances in media have **impacted how we relate to famous people** (such as with The Queen in popular TV series The Crown), and how that extends to our **relationships on social media**

- The impact of **technology on modern film & television**, such as with real-time VFX

- Why he **started writing** and **what he learned** from the responses

- If he thinks the **SF exodus** is overrated or underrated

- And more!
