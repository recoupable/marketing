# Every Design System — Reference Notes

Extracted from screenshots 2026-06-11. Companions: `../tenex/DESIGN.md` (the other dark-editorial reference), root `DESIGN.md` (Recoup's own system).

---

## The Core Concept

**A literary magazine that covers the future.** Where Tenex is a manifesto poster, Every is The New Yorker rebuilt for AI: serif masthead, columnists, beats, editorial illustration commissioned per story. The design says "we are writers and thinkers first" — which is exactly what makes their product and consulting arms credible. The site is a *publication you browse*, not a funnel you fall through (the funnel hides inside the publication).

The lesson: **if your strategy is trust-through-editorial, the design must feel edited — every surface art-directed, every story illustrated, every section a named beat.**

## Palette

| Role | Color | Usage |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ground | Black `#000`-ish | The whole site. A gallery wall for illustrations. |
| Text | Warm white / cream | Serif headlines, body. |
| Action | Pale powder blue `~#BFE3F2` | THE accent: Subscribe button, "Get in touch," "Browse all events," consulting banner background, form CTAs. Soft, not alarm-colored — a literary blue. |
| Content color | Unrestricted, per illustration | Cobalt blues, acid yellow, magenta, orange — but *inside artwork frames only*. Chrome stays achromatic. |

- **Same rule as Recoup's "achromatic chrome":** UI is black/white; color lives in content (illustrations, app screenshots, stamps). Every proves this scales to a huge editorial surface.
- **One soft accent for every action.** Powder blue = clickable/transactional, sitewide, no exceptions spotted.

## Typography

1. **Serif display (masthead + headlines)** — the EVERY wordmark with its swash italic *E*; headlines in a bookish serif. Reads publication, not product.
2. **The swash-italic letter tic** — headlines randomly italicize/swash one initial letter: "Socrates as a 𝒮ervice," "After 𝒜utomation," "What Comes 𝒩ext," "Claude Code for 𝒫roduct Managers." A tiny, ownable signature that makes every headline feel set by hand. Cheap to systematize, instantly recognizable.
3. **Grotesque sans** — kickers ("EVERY STUDIO", "PUTTING AI TO WORK"), bylines, UI labels, body-adjacent metadata. All-caps kickers name the beat above every rail.
4. **Serif body** — article decks and card descriptions stay bookish.

## Illustration System (the crown jewel)

A single commissioned-feeling style across hundreds of stories:
- **Vintage collage / engraving:** 19th-century etchings, Greek statuary, and 1950s-print-ad figures collaged with robots, laptops, and geometric AI motifs. (Statue in sunglasses; muse with a laptop; robot shaking a human's engraved hand.)
- **Limited flat-color fields per piece** (cobalt, acid yellow, magenta, kraft) over B&W line art — color-blocked like screen prints.
- **Recurring cast:** humanoid robots + classical humans as colleagues — never menacing, often celebrating together (high-fives, raised arms). The visual thesis: AI as collaborator.
- **Texture:** halftone dots, scratchboard lines, paper grain. Anti-gradient, anti-3D-render — the exact opposite of generic AI-startup aesthetics.
- **The postage stamps:** products rendered as a stamp series (Read/Email/Speak/Listen/Write/Organize), perforated edges and postmarks — collectible, analog, systematic. Brand metaphor: dispatches/mail/correspondence (matches "What Comes Next... in your inbox").

Tenex commits to ONE metaphor (statues+tech); Every commits to ONE STYLE that can illustrate anything. A magazine needs the latter.

## Layout & Composition

- **Newspaper grid:** featured story + side rail above the fold; then horizontal beat rails (4-up card rows) with all-caps kicker + one-line dek + arrow ("EVERY STUDIO — Lessons from engineers shipping AI products →").
- **Beats as named rails:** Vibe Check, Context Window, Putting AI to Work, The Future of Programming, The New Rules of Writing, From Our Columnists, Podcast. Each beat = a promise the reader learns to seek out.
- **The universe widget:** persistent bottom-right expandable panel ("EXPLORE THE EVERY UNIVERSE") cross-linking all surfaces + "Create your free account" — the conglomerate's nav stowed in a corner of every page.
- **Product cards:** app screenshot up top, name + one-liner + "Try it" pill — store-shelf simple, sitting inside editorial chrome.
- **Stats as plaques:** consulting metrics ($175B+ AUM / 16K+ / 130K+) in thin-bordered boxes with tiny engraved medal/star/envelope icons — credentials rendered as heraldry.
- **Forms stay in-world:** consulting intake flanked by two facing statues; inputs on dark cards; powder-blue submit.

## Motion & Interaction (inferred)

- Carousels with chevrons on testimonial/guide rails.
- Dismissable top banner for the current campaign (Fable 5 Vibe Check) — newsroom urgency surfaced as UI.
- Paywall modal: checklist + single email field + black "Unlock the Every universe" button; "Maybe later" as quiet text link. Polite, confident gate.

## What Recoup Should Take

1. **Beats with names.** Recurring rails ("Release Radar," "Fan Data Files," "Agent Diaries") train return visits better than a generic blog feed.
2. **The swash-letter tic** — one systematized typographic signature makes everything feel art-directed. We have the pixel-font equivalent; use it with that level of consistency.
3. **One illustration style, any subject.** Commission/generate within a single visual language (engraving + flat color blocks would suit "record label heritage meets agents" perfectly — sits right next to our shadow-as-border, achromatic chrome rules).
4. **Soft single accent for all actions.** Their powder blue = our equivalent; color signals "clickable," nothing else.
5. **Stamp-series productization.** Recoup workflows as a collectible series (one verb per stamp: Release, Research, Pitch, Track) — instant brand system for the plugin's contents.
6. **Universe widget.** When the surface count grows (chat, plugin, skills repo, newsletter, camps), one persistent cross-sell panel beats a mega-menu.
7. **Stats as engraved plaques** for the label-services page.
