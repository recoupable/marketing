## The Business in One Paragraph

High-ticket AI consultancy run like a media company. Two services (AI Transformation = strategy + change management; AI Engineering = outcome-priced dev squads), sold through a content engine: YouTube-craft video playbooks + a 40k-subscriber newsletter (ultrathink), all funneling into a revenue-qualified lead form. Founder credibility (Alex Lieberman / Morning Brew) does the cold-start distribution. The product is trust; the content manufactures it at scale.

## The Funnel

```
YouTube-grade playbooks + ultrathink newsletter (free, weekly, tactical)
   ↓
Brand site that sells a worldview ("win the next decade", "disrupt yourself")
   ↓
Service pages that disqualify and differentiate (anti-consultancy positioning)
   ↓
"Get Started" form with Annual Revenue dropdown → qualified sales conversation
```

Versus pm-world: same teach-publicly engine, opposite monetization. George converts readers to a $99 zip; Tenex converts execs to enterprise retainers. Recoup can run both: plugin for self-serve, this motion for labels/enterprise.

## Tactics Worth Stealing

1. **Sell the era, not the service.** Every page frames a historical transition (cost of intelligence → zero; AI-absent → AI-native) and positions the company as the guide through it. The service is almost incidental to the worldview.
2. **Invented spectrum vocabulary.** "AI-absent / AI-integrated / AI-native / AI-first" gives prospects words to locate themselves — and every location except the end-state implies they need help.
3. **Binary stakes + loss aversion.** "Disrupt yourself. Or be disrupted." "AI isn't scary. Ignoring it is." "Stay on the right side of history." Same blade, three angles.
4. **The honest bet.** "You could drive that transformation from within. Our bet is, you won't." Calling the prospect's bluff reads as confidence, not insult, because it's immediately softened ("Not because you don't want to...").
5. **Anti-consultancy positioning from inside consulting.** "No 6-month strategy work. No 200-slide presentations." Attack the category's known sins to exempt yourself.
6. **Incentive design as copy.** "We charge on outcomes / We pay on outcomes" — pricing mechanics presented as belief system.
7. **Founder-as-media-engine.** Lieberman's Morning Brew audience + YouTube-craft packaging means the consultancy gets media-company CAC. The playbook thumbnails are legitimately better than most creators'.
8. **One repeated closer.** "Stay on the right side of history" + muses ends every page. Brand mantra > rotating CTAs.
9. **Qualification as funnel feature.** Revenue dropdown on the form keeps the calendar clean — opposite of pm-world's frictionless $99 checkout, correct for the price point.
