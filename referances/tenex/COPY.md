## The Narrative Spine

Tenex doesn't sell consulting. It sells a **position in history**. The whole site is one argument in four moves:

1. **The era claim** — something irreversible is happening:
   > "As the cost of intelligence approaches zero, businesses will need to transition from AI-absent to AI-native if they want to win the next decade."
2. **The binary** — there are only two outcomes, and you choose:
   > "You have a choice. Disrupt yourself. Or be disrupted by others."
3. **The honest diagnosis** — you won't do it alone, and here's why (stated with empathy, not contempt):
   > "You could drive that transformation from within. Our bet is, you won't. Not because you don't want to. Not because you don't believe it's necessary. But because you're focused on business success, today."
4. **The destiny CTA** — act now and be remembered well:
   > "Stay on the right side of history."

Everything else (offers, FAQs, testimonials) hangs off this spine. **Lesson: write the argument first; pages are just the argument cut into rooms.**

## Voice Attributes

- **Declarative.** Short sentences. Full stops as percussion. "No 6-month strategy work. No 200-slide presentations. We get right to the punch line."
- **Stakes-maximal, fear-minimal.** The threat is named but the reader is never mocked: "AI isn't scary. Ignoring it is."
- **Self-assured to the edge of cocky, backed by mechanics.** "Our PMs and engineers are unicorns. Technically gifted. AI maximalists." — then immediately: outcome pricing, story points, 24-hour grading. Swagger, then receipts.
- **Plain-spoken about money and incentives.** "You pay for features delivered, not hours logged." "We pay our team based on the story-points that they complete. So everyone is aligned on output and speed."
- **A little snark, on purpose.** The newsletter promises "Some snark. All signal."
- **Empathetic candor as a trust move.** "The only thing more dangerous than ignoring AI? Implementing it, but forgetting about your people."

## Copy Mechanics (with examples)

### 1. The two-beat reversal
Setup in neutral voice, payoff flips the frame. The payoff gets the serif italic / yellow treatment in design.
> "You have a choice. **Disrupt yourself.** Or be disrupted by others."
> "AI isn't scary. **Ignoring it is.**"
> "Show, Then Tell" (playbook title)

### 2. Invented spectrum vocabulary
Coin the axis, own the axis. Prospects self-locate, and every location short of the end-state needs you.
> "shift from **AI-absent** to **AI-native**"
> "There are three types of relationships businesses will have with AI: **AI-absent, AI-integrated, and AI-first**."

### 3. Category-sin denial
Open an offer by listing the things everyone hates about the category, then negate them.
> "**No** 6-month strategy work. **No** 200-slide presentations."
> "Most consultancies overcharge, are under-qualified, and don't marry strategy with execution."

### 4. Role-replacement framing
Sell the service as a person you'd otherwise hire.
> "**We are your Chief AI Officer.**"
> "They're like having an internal engineering team. I can text them whenever." (client quote doing the same job)

### 5. Metaphor with teeth
One vivid concrete image per concept, never a pile of them.
> "We are **bomb-sniffing dogs** for your business."
> "your **unfair advantage** in the **AI arms race**"

### 6. The verbed brand
> "Ready to **Tenex** your business?"

### 7. Numbers as character witnesses
> "one world-class engineer can do more than 10 mediocre engineers, so we only hire the best"
> "3x more enterprise features with Mag7-level code quality"
> "saved $2M and turned a 20-person SDR team into 2"
> "40,000+ execs and builders read ultrathink"

### 8. Belief-list as offer page
Headline "What we believe", then convictions with operational consequences — pricing model presented as ideology:
> "We hire the best / We use AI (a lot) / We charge on outcomes / We pay on outcomes"

### 9. Curiosity-gap content titles (playbooks)
> "Your Google Traffic Has an Expiration Date. Here's What Replaces It."
> "Build an AI Agent That Replaces 90% of Your Sales Team"
> "How to Ship Software Without Touching Your Keyboard (Seriously)"
> "How to Test Your AI Before It Embarrasses You"
Formula: named authority + concrete artifact + outsized but specific outcome ("How Vercel's GTM Engineering Director built a weekend project that saved $2M").

### 10. Destiny language for CTAs and recruiting
> "Stay on the right side of history."
> "Come make history... This is your moment. Don't let it pass you by."
> "Win the next decade."

## Reusable Rules

1. **Argument before pages.** Era claim → binary → honest diagnosis → destiny CTA. If a section doesn't advance the argument, cut it.
2. **One enemy: inaction.** Never competitor-bashing by name; the villain is standing still. ("Ignoring it is.")
3. **Stakes in the headline, mechanics in the body.** Headlines sell history; body copy sells story points, sprint cycles, Slack channels, salary bands.
4. **Every superlative needs a receipt within one sentence.**
5. **Coin your vocabulary and repeat it verbatim** across pages (AI-native, win the next decade, right side of history). Repetition makes it the prospect's vocabulary too.
6. **Punctuate for percussion.** Fragments. Full stops. Then one long sentence when the idea needs room.
7. **Be honest about the uncomfortable thing** (you won't do this internally; people get forgotten in transformations) — candor is the trust accelerant.
8. **Snark is seasoning, not the dish.**
