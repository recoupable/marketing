## The Core Problem (Copy Blocks)

### For Artists

> Artists and music companies are drowning in operational work — marketing, content, analytics, catalog management. Generic AI tools produce "slop" that isn't postable. Hiring is expensive and doesn't scale.

**Pain language (from real users and customers):**
- "I got into music. I'm stuck in ops."
- "Every hat: creator, A&R, marketer, analyst."
- "The algorithm punishes silence but creating content takes forever."
- "I'd rather be in the studio, not on Canva."
- "Engagement is down."
- "Too many platforms."
- "Content backlog."

### For Labels / Enterprise

**CEO problems (why they write $5-10k/month checks):**

1. **"Can't scale without hiring."** Growth = headcount. $5-10k/mo for agents vs. $15-25k/mo in salary.
2. **"Operations are fragmented."** Spreadsheets, dashboards, Google Docs. Nothing works together.
3. **"Content doesn't scale."** 120,000 tracks uploaded to DSPs every day. Can't make quality content for every artist.
4. **"AI is coming, I'm not ready."** Want to be AI-native but staff won't adopt.
5. **"Deal flow is too slow."** Catalog acquirers: manual due diligence takes days. Should take hours.

**Staff problems (why they leave — things to address, not promote):**
- "More work, not less" — if it's buggier than ChatGPT, they'll use ChatGPT
- "I don't know what to do" — the "now what?" problem after first use
- "Results aren't good enough to show my boss"
- "I just need one thing" — staff wants a tool for their specific job, not a whole platform

### The Gap We're Closing

> CEO buys "imagine if your label was run by code." Staff needs "a tool that does one thing so well I'd never go back."

## Differentiation (Us vs. Them)

### Competitive Positioning Table

| Us | Them |
|----|------|
| **System** that runs music businesses | Chatbot or single tool |
| AI agents that **execute** autonomously | AI tools that recommend — you still do the work |
| Built for **music** — releases, fans, workflows | Generic tools adapted for music |
| **Multiple entry points** (web, Slack, email, CLI) = one system | Point solutions or separate products |
| Deep **artist context** (songs, face guide, brand docs) | Generic prompts with no memory |
| **Enterprise-ready** ($5-10k/month contracts) | B2C only, $0-40/month ceiling |

### The Moat (Why Not Just Use ChatGPT?)

> General purpose agents can't do what we do because they don't know the artist, the catalog, the audience, or the history. Our edge is **deep context** — the artist context system (artist.md, songs/, face-guide.png, audience.md) makes every tool artist-specific. Not a generic tool with a music skin.

**The bar for every feature:** Would a customer choose Recoup over doing it themselves or using ChatGPT?

### Named Competitors and Where We Win

| Competitor | What they do | Our advantage |
|------------|-------------|---------------|
| CoManager | 8 named AI managers. Clean UX. Recommends actions. | We execute, not just recommend. Enterprise contracts. Deep context. |
| Spincast | "AI That Runs Your Record Label." Label ops + social content. | Infrastructure play (API, CLI, MCP). Enterprise deals. Content pipeline depth. |
| Reverba | Claims autonomous artist management. | Unproven. We have paying enterprise customers. |
| Generic AI (ChatGPT, Claude) | Can do anything, knows nothing about your artist. | Deep artist context. Music-specific tools. One system, not per-conversation. |

## What Recoupable Is NOT

Use these as objection-handling copy or "we're not that" clarifications:

- **Not a chatbot.** Agents execute, not just answer questions.
- **Not a social media scheduler.** We create AND execute — content, strategy, distribution.
- **Not a record label or distributor.** We enable those who run them (while also running our own).
- **Not a tool or feature bundle.** One system. Agents run it.
- **Web, Slack, email, CLI are entry points** into the same system — not separate products.

## The Product Vision (Star-Rating Ladder)

Use this to paint a picture of where the product is going. Good for "Vision" or "Roadmap" pages.

### Content Pipeline (The PMF Feature)

| Level | Experience |
|-------|-----------|
| Today | Generates a video for one artist with one template |
| Next | Works for any artist. Multiple content types. Output is good enough to post. |
| Then | Auto-posts to the artist's socials. No download-and-reupload. |
| After | Tracks what performs. Shows "this one got 10x views — here's why." |
| Vision | Proactively generates content based on trending topics + artist context. You wake up to content ready to approve. |
| Dream | Artist's career visibly grows. System shows attribution: "Your streams went up 40% this month. Here's the content that drove it." |

### How It Works (3-Step Simplification)

1. **Connect your data.** Catalog, socials, distribution. One pipe. No chasing.
2. **Set the direction.** Releases, marketing, growth. You call the shots. Agents get the brief.
3. **Agents run it.** They execute. You create.

## Tone & Language Notes

From strategy docs — real language that resonates:

### Words That Work
- "Agents run it" (not "AI-powered")
- "System" (not "platform" or "tool")
- "One pipe" (for data integration)
- "Deep context" (our moat)
- "Hustle by default. Systems by design." (problem framing)
- "Infrastructure. Outcomes." (solution framing)
- "You create. Agents run the rest."
- "Music businesses are becoming autonomous."

### Words to Avoid
- "Revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "game-changing," "disruptive"
- "Leverage" (say "use")
- "AI artist" or "AI music" (say "AI-driven" or "AI-powered")
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- Exclamation marks in headlines or body copy

### The Founder's Voice (For Quotes, About Pages)

> "There's 1000 things to do." — Sidney on focus
> "I think that's going to be the feature that we find product market fit on." — Sidney on content pipeline
> "We run our label on it." — Sidney on dogfooding
> "The same loop that works for code works for music." — Sidney on the thesis
