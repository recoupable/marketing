# Voice & Tone

> Updated: 2026-03-16

## Core Voice

Recoupable sounds like a smart, no-BS friend who works in music tech. Not a corporation. Not a hype machine. Someone who's been in the studio at 2am AND understands marketing analytics.

## Rules

### ✅ DO

- **Show, don't tell** — Use specific examples, numbers, and stories instead of adjectives.
  WHY: "22 videos in 2 hours" is more convincing than "incredibly fast." Specifics build trust; adjectives trigger skepticism.

- **Use "AI-driven" or "AI-powered"** (not "AI artist" or "AI music")
  WHY: "AI artist" implies the AI is the creative. It triggered backlash from industry people who felt their craft was being replaced. "AI-driven" keeps the human in frame — the artist has intent, the AI executes.

- **Write in first person when telling stories** — "I sat down at 10pm..." not "Users can..."
  WHY: First person creates intimacy and trust. "Users can" sounds like a product manual. We're building a relationship, not documenting software.

- **Be specific about time and effort saved** — "22 videos in 2 hours" not "saves time"
  WHY: Vague claims ("saves time") are ignored. Specific claims ("22 videos in 2 hours") are memorable and shareable.

- **Use short sentences. Short paragraphs.** One idea per paragraph.
  WHY: Web readers scan. Long paragraphs get skipped. Short = readable = retained.

- **Use active voice** — "The agent creates 20 posts" not "20 posts are created by the agent"
  WHY: Active voice is shorter, clearer, and more engaging. Passive voice is bureaucratic and boring.

### ❌ DON'T

- **Never use "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "game-changing," or "disruptive"**
  WHY: These are marketing clichés that signal "we have nothing real to say." They've been so overused that readers' eyes literally skip them. Say what the product actually does instead.

- **Never use exclamation marks in headlines or body copy**
  WHY: Exclamation marks signal insecurity — like shouting because you're not sure people are listening. Confidence is quiet.

- **Never say "leverage" — say "use"**
  WHY: "Leverage" is corporate jargon. "Use" is human. We talk like people, not consultants.

- **Never start with "In today's fast-paced world..."**
  WHY: This is the opening line of every generic AI-written blog post. It signals that no thought went into the intro. Start with something specific.

- **Never make claims without proof** — no "best in class" without data
  WHY: Unsubstantiated superlatives damage credibility. If you can't prove it, don't say it.

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Blog posts | Conversational, knowledgeable, curious |
| Homepage | Confident, concise, benefit-focused |
| Social posts | Casual, hook-driven, platform-native |
| Email | Warm, direct, one clear CTA |
| Product copy | Clear, precise, no fluff |
