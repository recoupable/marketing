# Design Context

> Persisted for Impeccable skills (frontend-design, colorize, polish, adapt, delight).
> Source: brand docs, website-structure-report, AGENTS.md.

## Users

- **Primary:** Music industry operators — artists, label staff, distributors, catalog owners — who want to run their business with less manual work and more automation.
- **Secondary:** Developers and AI builders integrating with music infrastructure.
- **Context:** They're evaluating a serious platform, not a toy. They need confidence that Recoup is the system serious teams use. They may be skeptical of "AI" hype.
- **Job to be done:** Understand what Recoup is, who it's for, and take the next step (sign up, read, subscribe).

## Brand Personality

- **Three words:** Confident, no-BS, infrastructure.
- **Voice:** Smart friend in music tech. Not corporate, not hype. Show don't tell; specifics over adjectives; active voice; short sentences.
- **Emotional goals:** Trust, clarity, "this is the future of music ops" — not playful or whimsical. Calm confidence.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Tone:** Editorial, refined, confident. Music + tech infrastructure. Avoid generic SaaS (purple gradients, centered hero metric, card grids that look like every AI site).
- **References:** Serious product/marketing sites with strong typography and restrained color. Think Stripe, Linear, or high-end editorial — not consumer app.
- **Anti-references:** No "AI slop" — no cyan-on-dark, purple-blue gradients, neon accents, rounded cards with one colored border, centered everything, identical card grids.
- **Theme:** Light mode primary. Use brand colors from project config or `public/brand` assets; do not assume a specific hex. Tinted neutrals; one accent for CTAs and key UI.
- **Differentiation:** Typography and spatial rhythm should feel intentional and memorable; someone should ask "how was this made?" not "which AI made this?"

## Design Principles

1. **Hierarchy through typography and space** — Varied spacing (tight groupings, generous separations), fluid type scale, left-aligned or asymmetric layouts where it adds emphasis.
2. **Color with purpose** — Use project brand colors for CTAs and key links (from config or brand assets). Tinted neutrals (no pure gray). No gray text on colored backgrounds; use shade of background or transparency.
3. **No generic containers** — Not everything in a card. Break the grid intentionally; avoid identical card grids (icon + heading + text repeated).
4. **Confidence over decoration** — No gradient text for impact, no glassmorphism, no bounce easing. Motion (if any) is subtle and purposeful (e.g. stagger on load, ease-out).
5. **Accessible and robust** — WCAG contrast, focus indicators, touch targets 44px where needed, semantic HTML. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
