# User Journey — Label GM / Operator (non-technical)

> First visit to recoupable.com. Arrived from a LinkedIn post a friend tagged me in. Reading top to bottom, reacting honestly.

## Who I am

I'm the GM at an independent label — I also do a lot of the day-to-day that a management company would do. On any given week I'm running A&R, signing off on release calendars, chasing our marketing person about content, writing playlist pitches, and trying to make sense of royalty statements and catalog questions when an investor or a distributor asks. My team is four people doing the work of ten. I use ChatGPT and Claude the way everyone does now — to draft emails, summarize a contract, rewrite a bio. I am NOT technical. I don't code. When someone says "API" or "MCP" or tells me to run something in a "terminal," my eyes glaze and I assume that part isn't for me. I'm genuinely curious whether AI can take real work off my plate, but I've been burned by hype and I can smell AI slop a mile away.

## What I want (one sentence)

Show me, in plain English, that AI can actually DO label work I recognize — release plans, content, pitching, catalog/diligence — and give me one easy, non-scary way to start.

## 5-second test

**Pass, mostly.** The H1 "**Put AI to work inside your music business.**" is exactly the promise I want, in my language. The eyebrow "**Building the agentic music industry**" — "agentic" is a word I've heard but couldn't define for you, so it reads as a little buzzwordy. The subhead "**research lab and implementation partner for labels, catalogs, and platforms — from strategy to the custom agents that run in your stack**" half-lands: "for labels" = me, "strategy" = good, but "research lab" makes me wonder if these are academics, and "custom agents that run in your stack" lost me on "stack." Still — within 5 seconds I know it's music + AI + for labels, and there's a friendly "**Talk to us**" button. I'd keep scrolling.

## Section-by-section

### Header (chrome)
- **Thinking:** Clean. "Research" / "Consulting" and a black "Talk to us" pill. Minimal, not intimidating.
- **Narrative so far:** This looks like a real company, not a sketchy tool.
- **Friction:** "Research" opens a new tab to an external site — I'm not here to read a journal, so I ignore it. Only two nav items means I can't tell from the top what I'd actually *get*. No "For Labels," no "Pricing," no "Results" up top.
- **Works:** "Talk to us" is everywhere and low-pressure. Good for a human like me.
- **Doesn't:** Nothing actively breaks. It just doesn't tell a non-technical buyer where to go besides "talk to a person."

### 1. Hero
- **Thinking:** "Put AI to work inside your music business" — yes, that's the thing I want.
- **Narrative so far:** Right industry, right promise, right tone.
- **Friction:** "research lab," "agents," "your stack." I don't have a "stack." I have Google Drive, a distributor portal, a spreadsheet, and a group chat.
- **Works:** "**Talk to us**" → I'd click that. The H1 + "for labels" makes me feel addressed.
- **Doesn't:** "Read our research" as the secondary CTA is aimed at a different person than me. I'd never click it first.

### 2. Logos — "Used by teams at"
- **Thinking:** Oh — Atlantic, Warner, 300, ONErpm, Duetti… I *recognize* most of these.
- **Narrative so far:** Trust just went up a notch. If Atlantic and Warner touch this, it's not vaporware.
- **Friction:** A couple I don't know (Fat Beats, Big Ass Kids), and "Used by teams at" is soft — does that mean a customer, or one person tried it once? I'd want a logo to be clickable to a story.
- **Works:** Recognizable major-label logos are the single most reassuring thing on the page for me.
- **Doesn't:** No proof behind the logos. *(Logos need live verification — parent will confirm these are real/permitted.)*

### 3. Lanes — Research / Build / Partner
- **Thinking:** Three columns explaining how they work.
- **Narrative so far:** "Partner — We implement it with you… strategy, rollout, and custom agents built for your roster and workflows" — THAT one is me.
- **Friction:** The "Build" lane — "**Open skills, plugins, and an API so your team can put music intelligence into Claude, Cursor, and your own stack**" — "plugins, API, Cursor, stack" tells me this column is for a developer, not me. "Cursor" I've never heard of.
- **Works:** Splitting "Partner / Talk to us" out so I can self-select into the non-technical lane is smart. I'd head for that.
- **Doesn't:** Two of three lanes (Research, Build) are aimed past me. As a first-time label buyer it can feel like I'm only 1/3 of the audience.

### 4. Research — "We learn in the open, with real operators."
- **Thinking:** There's a little artist dashboard card (Gatsby Grace — monthly listeners, saves, top cities, playlist placements).
- **Narrative so far:** The *card* is the best thing in this section — it looks exactly like the kind of artist snapshot I'd kill to have auto-generated.
- **Friction:** The headline and copy ("we publish what actually works… as we build with labels and catalogs") is about *them publishing*, not about *me getting that dashboard*. I cared about the picture, not the blog.
- **Works:** The ResearchCard visual is concrete and relatable — real metrics, real artist framing.
- **Doesn't:** It's filed under "Research" with a "Read the latest" link to an external blog, so the one genuinely exciting visual is wrapped in a "go read our writing" ask.

### 5. Problem — "Your team has Claude. It still can't do the work."
- **Thinking:** Hmm — *do* we have Claude? One of us has a ChatGPT login and I dabble in Claude. "Your team has Claude" assumes more than is true for us.
- **Narrative so far:** But the three bullets nail it: "**It doesn't know your roster, catalog, or deal terms**," "**It can't touch Drive, royalty data, or distributors on its own**," "**Diligence, A&R, content ops — none of it built in.**" Yes. That is *exactly* why ChatGPT has been disappointing for real label work.
- **Friction:** The headline leads with "Claude" specifically — feels slightly presumptuous and a touch technical. I'd have said "Your team has ChatGPT."
- **Works:** This is the section where I went "they actually understand my job." Roster, deal terms, royalty data, distributors — that's my world.
- **Doesn't:** Naming one specific AI tool I might not use risks a "well, not us" shrug at the headline before I read the (excellent) bullets.

### 6. Build — dark section, "One harness, many workflows."
- **Thinking:** Whoa, it went dark and technical. Little chips: "Claude · Cursor · API · MCP · Chat." A code window appears with `npm install -g @recoupable/cli` and `recoup research "Billie Eilish"`.
- **Narrative so far:** This is the moment I start to feel like the page stopped talking to me. "**One harness**" — I do not know what a harness is. "API," "MCP," "Cursor," "CLI," a black terminal with `$` commands — that's the screen my developer friends share, not me.
- **Friction:** High. Maybe 60% of this section is over my head. The terminal tab is the default view and it's the scariest one.
- **Works:** When it cycles to the **Claude** and **ChatGPT** tabs, I recognize those chat windows, and the *output* is thrilling — "98.2M monthly listeners… Generated 12 assets — 8 short-form videos, 3 covers, 1 press kit… All tasks complete · 2m 14s." THAT outcome is what I want. If the section led with that result instead of `npm install`, I'd be sold.
- **Doesn't:** The framing ("harness," "MCP," terminal-first) makes a non-technical operator feel this product is really for engineers. I almost bounced here.

### 7. Open tools shelf — "The tools we build, free to install."
- **Thinking:** Pretty animated book spines: Agentic Diligence, A&R, Content, Operator, Fan.
- **Narrative so far:** The *names* are perfect — Diligence, A&R, Content, Operator, Fan is literally my org chart. For a second I'm excited.
- **Friction:** Then the install box says `npx skills add recoupable/diligence`. I have no idea what `npx` is, where I'd type it, or whether I'd break something. "open source" / "install in seconds" — not for me, it isn't. There's no "what does this actually do for me" or "no-code way to try it."
- **Works:** The pack taxonomy (especially "Operator — the day-to-day of running a label") tells me they've mapped real label functions.
- **Doesn't:** Every single pack's call-to-action is a developer command. The most relatable naming on the page is gated behind the most intimidating instruction on the page. I'd look, admire, and move on without touching it.

### 8. Consulting band — "Start executing it."
- **Thinking:** "**Most music teams are still planning their AI strategy. Start executing it.**"
- **Narrative so far:** Back in my lane. "**Training and implementation from operators who build the tools — not slide decks**" is a great line — it tells me I get hands-on help, not a McKinsey deck.
- **Friction:** Almost none. Maybe "inside their own stack" again, but by now I read past it.
- **Works:** This is the clearest "we'll do it *with* you" message, and the "Talk to us" button is right there. This is where a non-technical buyer like me re-engages after the scary middle.
- **Doesn't:** It's a little late on the page — I had to survive sections 6 and 7 to get here.

### 9. Pull quote — anonymized
- **Thinking:** "**Catalog diligence is one of the biggest pain points I have. Cut it down to minutes and it changes how we buy.**" — *Catalog fund operator.*
- **Narrative so far:** Diligence *is* a pain for me too when an investor circles our catalog, so it partly speaks to me.
- **Friction:** It's attributed to a "catalog fund operator," i.e., a *buyer*, not a label GM. And it's anonymous. A real name + label would land 3x harder; anonymous quotes read as "we couldn't get permission."
- **Works:** The outcome ("minutes," "changes how we buy") is tangible.
- **Doesn't:** Wrong persona for me and no attribution — I half-believe it.

### 10. Proof — "We run our own labels."
- **Thinking:** "**Recoup Records and our artist Gatsby Grace use these same tools every day — to write release plans, audit catalogs, pitch playlists, and run the business.**"
- **Narrative so far:** This is the single most relatable paragraph on the page. Release plans, catalog audits, playlist pitching, running the business — that is my literal to-do list. "Every skill we publish earns its keep on a real roster first" is a strong trust line.
- **Friction:** The link says "**See how we dogfood**" — "dogfood" is internal tech slang; I had to guess it means "use it ourselves." Just say "See how we run our own label."
- **Works:** "We use it on our own label and artist" is exactly the proof a skeptic wants.
- **Doesn't:** It *promises* proof but (see below) the page it links to doesn't deliver it.

### 11. Final CTA — "Let's build it in your stack."
- **Thinking:** Big dark closer.
- **Narrative so far:** Strong, confident.
- **Friction:** "**in your stack**" — for the third time, this phrase means nothing to me. I'd have written "in your business" or "with your team."
- **Works:** "Talk to us" one more time. Easy, human exit.
- **Doesn't:** The headline subtly re-frames the whole thing as a *build* (engineering) project right at the goodbye, which slightly undercuts the "we'll do it with you" feeling I just got from the consulting band.

### Footer (chrome)
- **Thinking:** Now I see the real map: Consulting, Research, Recoup Records, Contact / Platform, Developers, API Docs, Pricing / About, Vision, Open app.
- **Narrative so far:** Ah — there's **Pricing** and a **Platform** page after all; they were just hidden from the top nav.
- **Friction:** I had to reach the very bottom to find Pricing and Platform. "Developers" and "API Docs" confirm a big chunk of this company is aimed at technical people. No "For Labels," no "Case Studies / Results," no "Book a call."
- **Works:** Contact email is there; the footer is organized.
- **Doesn't:** The things a non-technical buyer most wants (pricing, proof/results, a labels page) are either buried or absent from the journey.

## Jargon that lost me (specific words/sections)

- **"agentic"** (eyebrow, and everywhere) — heard it, can't define it.
- **"your stack"** (hero, consulting, final CTA) — I don't have a "stack." Used 3x.
- **"custom agents"** / **"agents"** (hero, lanes, build) — vaguely ominous; I'm not sure what an "agent" does vs. a chatbot.
- **"One harness"** (§6) — total mystery word.
- **"API," "MCP," "CLI," "Cursor," "plugins," "npm install," `npx skills add`** (§3 Build lane, §6, §7) — flatly signals "engineers only."
- **"open source," "install in seconds"** (§7) — not reassuring to me; I can't install anything from a command line.
- **"dogfood"** (§10) — internal tech slang.
- **"research lab"** (hero) — makes me wonder if I'm the customer or a case study.

## Links I clicked (expectation vs reality)

1. **"Talk to us" → /consulting** (clicked from header, hero, and consulting band)
   - **Expectation:** A simple "tell us about your label, book a call."
   - **Reality:** Strong page *for me.* H1 "AI Strategy for Music Companies," big credentials (10+ platinum, Beyoncé/Nicki credits, $2.5M D2F, US patent), a "**Who This Is For**" grid that literally lists **Labels** and **Management Companies**, transparent pricing ("**Sessions start at $500**"), and an FAQ that answers my #1 fear: "**Do I need technical expertise on my team? No.**" This page rescues the whole experience for a non-technical buyer.
   - **Mismatch:** The buttons are mailto links ("Book a Call" / "Get in Touch" open an email), not an actual calendar/booking — slightly more friction than "Book a Call" implies. There's an `/advisory/book` route elsewhere that sounds like real booking but isn't linked here.

2. **"See the tools" → /platform**
   - **Expectation:** I'll *see the tools* — screenshots, a demo, what they do.
   - **Reality:** A thin text page: headings "Agents / Workflows / Integrations / Data Layer / **API & CLI**" with one terse line each ("Not a chatbot. Not a feature bundle."). No screenshots, no demo, and it ends with "**Get started**" linking out to the chat app. **Anchor/promise mismatch** — "See the tools" showed me almost nothing, and the one technical heading (API & CLI) reminds me again this isn't built for my hands.

3. **"See how we dogfood" → /company/recoup-records** (the "proof" page — my most-wanted click)
   - **Expectation:** Proof. Numbers. "Here's a release we ran, here's what the agents produced, here's the growth."
   - **Reality:** Three sentences. "Our label runs on this system." + "Every release, marketing push, content run: agents execute. Same platform we sell. That's how we know it works." + "**Real outputs. Real growth loops. Real proof.**" — but it *names* proof without *showing* any. No screenshots, no Gatsby Grace numbers, no release example. **Biggest letdown of the visit:** the homepage promised the most relatable thing and the destination is a stub.

3b. **Dead-end / duplicate I noticed:** there's also a `/records` route that's just a "**Coming soon**" page — a near-duplicate of `/company/recoup-records`. If I ever landed there from a search or a stray link, it'd read as broken/abandoned. Pick one URL.

4. **External links I did NOT click but noted:** "Read our research" → research.recoupable.com, "Open app" / "Get started" → chat.recoupable.com, "API Docs" → developers.recoupable.com, and all the `npx skills add recoupable/*` commands. *(All need live verification — parent will check.)* For a non-technical reader, "Read our research," "API Docs," and the `npx` commands set a clearly developer/academic expectation, not a "try it as a label operator" one.

**Links I wanted but couldn't find:**
- A **"For Labels"** page (the consulting page has a labels blurb, but no dedicated landing).
- **Case studies / Results** — there's apparently a `/results` and `/solutions` route, but nothing on the homepage points me to real outcomes.
- **Pricing** — exists, but footer-only; I want it in the journey.
- A **no-code "try it" / demo / book-a-call calendar** — the closest is a mailto.

## Objections that would make me bounce

1. "This is built for engineers." The middle of the page (terminal, `npm install`, `npx`, MCP, API, "harness") makes me feel like the real product needs a developer I don't have.
2. "Where's the proof?" Recognizable logos and a great problem statement, but the dedicated proof page is empty and the testimonial is anonymous and from a *fund buyer*, not a label.
3. "Is this consulting, software, or a research blog?" Three lanes + research-lab framing makes me unsure what I'm actually buying.
4. "Do I have to install something?" The free tools all require a command line, so the "free, in the open" generosity doesn't translate into anything I can use.

## Verdict (convert? likelihood 1–10)

**6 / 10 — conditional.** I would *not* buy software off this page, and the technical middle nearly lost me. BUT the "Talk to us → /consulting" path is genuinely excellent for a non-technical operator — the credentials, the "Who This Is For: Labels," the "$500" transparency, and the "**No, you don't need technical expertise**" FAQ are exactly what I needed, and "Talk to us" is low-pressure. So I'd likely send the email. The score is held down by the developer-jargon stretch (§6–7), the empty proof page, and proof/pricing being buried. If I'd bounced at the dark "harness/npm" section before reaching the consulting band, this would be a 3.

## Top 5 fixes (ranked)

1. **Fill the proof page (`/company/recoup-records`).** It's my most-wanted click and it's a stub. Put real Recoup Records / Gatsby Grace artifacts there — a release plan the agents wrote, before/after growth, a content batch — and consolidate or kill the duplicate `/records` "Coming soon" page.
2. **De-jargon the buyer path.** Replace/translate "harness," "your stack," "MCP/API/CLI," "agentic," and "dogfood" in the main narrative. Lead §6 with the *outcome* ("12 ready-to-post assets in 2m 14s"), not `npm install`. Keep the developer stuff, but route it to a clearly-labeled "For developers" lane so non-technical buyers don't feel locked out.
3. **Give the free tools a no-code on-ramp.** Next to every `npx skills add …`, add a plain-English "what this does for a label" and a "Try it in the app / no install needed" option. Right now the most relatable section (Diligence/A&R/Content/Operator/Fan) is gated behind the most intimidating instruction.
4. **Make "See the tools" actually show tools, and put real proof + a Labels page in the journey.** The /platform page is text-only and under-delivers on its anchor; add screenshots/demo. Surface a "For Labels" page, Results/case studies, and Pricing somewhere above the footer.
5. **Upgrade the social proof.** Make the customer logos clickable to a story, and replace the anonymous "catalog fund operator" quote with an attributed quote from an actual label/management operator (my persona), so a skeptic believes it.
