# User Journey — Distribution / Creator-Tools Platform (Strategy & Partnerships)

> First-visit walkthrough of recoupable.com, in character. Source files read; reactions are to the rendered page top-to-bottom.

## Who I am

I run Strategy & Partnerships at a broad B2C creator-tools platform. We started in AI mastering, then bolted on distribution, sample packs, plugins, and an education arm — "everything to everybody" for music creators. I came up as a producer, so I speak the music part fluently and the API/margin part fluently. My CEO said "have a chat with Recoup, see if we can do something together." I'm not a label and I'm not a catalog. I'm a platform with an existing enterprise stack, real token-cost exposure, and a discoverability problem (we're horizontal, hard to market). I'm here to see whether Recoup is something we **partner with, build alongside, or build instead.**

## What I want (one sentence)

I want to know if Recoup offers a partner/embed path — API + open skills I can drop into our stack to give our creators an agentic overlay and a post-distribution promotion layer — and who pays for the tokens when an agent, not a human, is the customer.

## 5-second test

**Eyebrow:** "Building the agentic music industry." — *This is the line that made me lean in.* It's exactly the thesis I came to discuss (agentic overlay, agent discovery funnel). Best signal on the page for me.

**H1:** "Put AI to work inside your music business." — *Half-hit.* "Inside your music business" frames me as an operator who *runs* a music business. I don't run a music business; I run a platform that thousands of music businesses sit on top of. The word "your" quietly reassigns me to the wrong seat.

**Sub:** "...research lab and implementation partner for labels, catalogs, and platforms — from strategy to the custom agents that run in your stack." — *They said the magic word: "platforms," and "custom agents that run in your stack."* That's three for three on vocabulary. So in 5 seconds I believe Recoup *might* be for me. The open question I carry into the page: is "platforms" a real lane here, or just a word in a list?

**Verdict at 5s:** Curious, cautiously optimistic. The framing is "research + implementation partner," which reads as services, not as a partner/integration product. I'll go hunting for the platform/partner path.

## Section-by-section

### Header (chrome)

- **Thinking:** Nav is two items — "Research" (external) and "Consulting" — plus a black "Talk to us" button that also points to `/consulting`. That's it.
- **Narrative so far:** The entire top-of-funnel funnels to one place: consulting. There is no "Platform," "Developers," "Partners," or "For platforms" in the nav. As a partner-evaluator, I have nowhere obvious to go except "talk to a consultant."
- **Friction:** My lane (`/platform`, `/developers`, `/pricing`) is **footer-only** per the nav config. The header pretends Recoup is a research-and-consulting shop. If I'm scanning for "is there a product/API I can build on," the header tells me "no, book a call."
- **Works:** Minimal, clean, fast. "Research" being a first-class nav item supports the "learn in the open" thesis, which I respect as a partner.
- **Doesn't:** No partnership/platform entry point in the primary nav. Every signpost says "Talk to us → consulting." For a B2B partner motion that's a missed turn.

### 1. Hero

- **Thinking:** Covered in the 5-second test. Eyebrow nails my thesis; sub-headline explicitly names "platforms" and "custom agents that run in your stack."
- **Narrative so far:** Recoup is positioning as a research lab + implementation partner. Good vocabulary, ambiguous motion.
- **Friction:** Both CTAs are soft for me. "Talk to us" → `/consulting` (services). "Read our research" → external (top-of-funnel learning). Neither says "See the platform / API / partner with us."
- **Works:** "platforms" in the audience list; "implementation partner"; "run in your stack." This is the most partner-aware hero I've seen from a music-AI company.
- **Doesn't:** "Put AI to work inside **your** music business" subtly miscasts a platform as an operator. I'd want a hero variant or sub-line that says "embed it in **your product**" / "for the platforms creators already use."

### 2. Logos — "Used by teams at"

- **Thinking:** Atlantic, Warner, 300, Rostrum, Fat Beats, ONErpm, Duetti, Big Ass Kids. *Needs live verification (parent will check) — logo files load via `/api/customer-logos/<slug>`.*
- **Narrative so far:** Heavy label/distributor proof. ONErpm and Duetti are the only platform-ish/distribution-ish names; the rest are labels/catalogs.
- **Friction:** I don't see a peer. No mastering tool, no DAW, no plugin marketplace, no horizontal creator platform. The proof says "labels trust us," not "platforms build on us." It quietly tells me I'd be the first of my kind here.
- **Works:** The names are credible and recognizable — strong trust for the label/catalog ICP.

### 3. Lanes — "Research in public. Tools in the open. Implementation with your team."

- **Thinking:** Three cards. Research → external. **Build "See the tools"** → `/platform`. Partner "Talk to us" → `/consulting`.
- **Narrative so far:** This is the clearest map of how Recoup works, and it's the first place I see a dedicated "Build" lane pointing at `/platform`. The middle card copy — "Open skills, plugins, and an API so your team can put music intelligence into Claude, Cursor, and **your own stack**" — is *exactly* my pitch.
- **Friction:** The third card is literally labeled **"Partner"** — "We implement it with you... strategy, rollout, and custom agents built for your roster and workflows." But it routes to `/consulting`, which (spoiler) is a 1:1 advisory page about a person, not a platform-partnership page. "Partner" here means "we consult for you," not "we integrate with you / co-build / rev-share." That's a meaning collision for someone in my role.
- **Works:** The "Build → See the tools" card is the right door for me and the copy is on-message. I'm clicking it.
- **Doesn't:** "Partner" the word is claimed by consulting. There's no fourth lane for "platforms / embed / OEM."

### 4. Research — "We learn in the open, with real operators."

- **Thinking:** Body: "Catalog diligence, label operations, agent rollouts — we publish what actually works... as we build with labels and catalogs." ResearchCard shows a Gatsby Grace artist analytics card.
- **Narrative so far:** Research lane is real and credible. But the topics — catalog diligence, label ops — are squarely label/catalog, not platform-integration.
- **Friction:** Nothing here speaks to platform concerns (agent discovery funnel, token economics, embedding, DSP fraud/slop). The ResearchCard is an artist dashboard — a feature demo, useful but it reinforces "operator tool," not "infrastructure I embed."
- **Works:** Publishing in the open is a genuine partner-trust signal. I'd actually read this.
- **Doesn't:** No research artifact aimed at platforms/developers. I want a piece like "who pays for agent tokens" or "embedding music intelligence" — that would be catnip.

### 5. Problem — "Your team has Claude. It still can't do the work."

- **Thinking:** Three gaps: "No music context" (roster, catalog, deal terms), "No safe access" (Drive, royalty data, distributors), "No music workflows" (diligence, A&R, content ops).
- **Narrative so far:** Sharp problem statement — and it's genuinely a platform-relevant insight (a raw LLM doesn't know music). This is the "why an agentic overlay needs domain grounding" argument, which I buy.
- **Friction:** Every example is an internal-ops frame: "your roster," "your catalog," "your deal terms," diligence/A&R. My users aren't my roster — they're millions of creators. The gap is framed for a team using Claude internally, not for a platform embedding music intelligence for end-users.
- **Works:** "No music context / no safe access / no music workflows" is a clean, true trichotomy. It's reusable language I might steal.
- **Doesn't:** Doesn't translate the gap to "your end-users' agents can't do music work either — plug us in." One sentence of platform framing would land it.

### 6. Build — "One harness, many workflows." (my lane — reacting hard)

- **Thinking:** Dark band. Chips: Claude / Cursor / API / MCP / Chat. Sub: "Chat is a hosted workspace. The API and open skills are how you embed music intelligence in Claude, Cursor, and your own tools — **bring your own agent, Recoup plugs in.**" ArchitectureDiagram cycles Terminal / Claude / ChatGPT showing `npm install -g @recoupable/cli`, `recoup research`, `recoup content create`.
- **Narrative so far:** **This is the section I came for.** "Bring your own agent, Recoup plugs in" + MCP + API + CLI + the surfaces shown (terminal, Claude, ChatGPT) is precisely the headless/embed future I'm planning. The CLI demo (`recoup research "Billie Eilish"`, batch content create) shows the primitive I'd wire into our product.
- **Friction:** It's a beautiful dead-end. There is **no CTA in this section.** No "Read the API docs," no "See integration guide," no "Talk to partnerships." It shows me the most partner-relevant thing on the site and then gives me nowhere to click. To act on it I have to scroll to the footer and find "Developers" / "API Docs" myself. Also: MCP is a chip but never explained — for me, "is there a Recoup MCP server I can point our agents at?" is a top-3 question and it's left implicit.
- **Works:** Multi-surface story (Claude/Cursor/ChatGPT/Terminal) proves model-agnostic embedding — exactly the "agent discovery funnel" neutrality I need. "Bring your own agent, Recoup plugs in" is the single best partner sentence on the page.
- **Doesn't:** No link, no MCP detail, no "who pays for tokens" / cost model, no integration depth. This section earns a click it never offers.

### 7. Open tools shelf — "The tools we build, free to install."

Also no "browse all skills / contribute / docs" link beyond the install string.
- **Works:** Open, installable, in-seconds — credible. The shelf interaction is delightful and the "see how we work" framing fits a partner doing diligence.
- **Doesn't:** No pack speaks to my use case (post-distribution promotion, discovery overlay). No link to a skills registry/repo to judge breadth.

## Objections that would make me bounce

1. **"There's no path for me."** Every CTA is consulting. `/platform` is a product page, not a partner page. I can't find "let's integrate" — only "let's book a call with Sidney."
2. **"Am I the customer or the partner?"** "Put AI to work inside your music business" + `/platform` CTA → chat app keep recasting me as an end-operator buying a SaaS seat, not a platform embedding infrastructure.
3. **"Token economics are unanswered."** Credits exist for the SaaS tiers, but nothing addresses who pays when an agent (not a human) is the buyer, per-call cost, or partner margins. That's a deal-defining gap for me.
4. **"Channel conflict."** They run their own labels/artists. Will they compete with my creators? No neutrality reassurance.
5. **"Depth isn't here."** `/developers` and `/platform` are blurbs. No MCP server, auth, rate limits, SLAs-for-partners, or integration guide to evaluate build-vs-buy.
6. **"No one like me is here."** All proof is labels/catalogs. Zero platform/OEM evidence.

## Verdict

**Would I convert (book the partnership conversation)? Probably yes — but as a consulting call, not a partnership, and slightly against the page's grain.**

The thesis-level fit is real: "Building the agentic music industry," "bring your own agent, Recoup plugs in," open skills, model-agnostic surfaces, API/CLI/MCP. That's enough that I'll take the call my CEO asked for. But the site is built for **labels and catalogs buying advisory**, not for **platforms negotiating an integration/partnership.** The strongest partner content (Build section) dead-ends, and the only "for platforms"-sounding route (`/platform`) underdelivers and routes to the consumer app. I'd email partnerships directly if such an address existed; instead I'll use the generic "Book a Call."

**Likelihood: 6/10.** (High intent from vocabulary fit; dragged down by no partner path, a dead-end build section, and unanswered token/partnership economics.)

## Top 5 fixes (ranked)

1. **Ship a real "For platforms / Partners" path** — a dedicated route + header/footer nav item that speaks to embedding, OEM/white-label, rev-share or usage-based partner pricing, the agent discovery funnel, and "who pays for tokens." Point the lanes "Partner" card and the "build it in your stack" CTA here, not at consulting.
2. **Give Section 6 (Build) a CTA.** It's the best partner content on the page and has zero links. Add "Read the API & MCP docs," "See an integration guide," and "Talk to partnerships." Explain the **MCP** chip — a partner-facing "point your agents at the Recoup MCP server" story.
3. **Fix `/platform`.** Rebuild it as an embed/partner page (integration model, MCP/API depth, partner tiers, a platform case study), or rename the current page so "Platform" doesn't read as "for platforms." Change the CTA from "Get started → chat app" to docs/partnership.
4. **Answer token + margin economics** for the agent-as-buyer model — a short section or page on per-call cost, partner margins, and who pays tokens in an agent-mediated funnel. This is deal-defining for any platform.
5. **Disambiguate "Partner" and add platform proof + neutrality.** Stop using "Partner" for consulting *and* an enterprise label tier. Add at least one platform/OEM logo or case study, and a one-liner on channel-conflict neutrality given Recoup runs its own labels.
