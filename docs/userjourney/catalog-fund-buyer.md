# User Journey — Catalog Fund Diligence Buyer

> First-visit walkthrough of recoupable.com, in character. Analysis only.
> Persona reading the rendered page top to bottom, reacting honestly.

## Who I am

I run a one-person catalog-valuation consultancy out of London — six years on my own, and before that I did asset-backed lending against music catalogs at a finance firm. I work solo. My one anchor client is a catalog fund deploying about $140M this year across ~140 catalogs, so I'm doing a *lot* of first-pass screens. My whole world is Microsoft — Power Query, Power BI, Excel. That's where the work actually happens.

My single worst moment is when someone says "can you do a quick valuation?" because nothing is quick. The seller sends three years of royalty statements in three different formats — or partial, or wrong — and I lose half a day just normalizing them before I can even ballpark income, top tracks, and vintage. *That* is the job I'd pay to make faster.

I subscribed to Claude this week. I am a total beginner. I do not know what a "plugin," "skill," "MCP," or "harness" is. I came here because a LinkedIn post mentioned a "catalog plugin." I'm sceptical of generic SaaS — there's enough info out there for me to do this myself. But I love the *idea* of a tool that becomes **my** version: local, tuned to my own curves and past decisions, and absolutely not training some vendor on my deal data.

## What I want (one sentence)

Convince me you can turn a messy pile of royalty statements into a fast, ballpark catalog summary (3–4 years of income, top tracks, vintage) — and that I can own and customise it without you seeing my data.

## 5-second test (first impression, pre-scroll)

**H1: "Put AI to work inside your music business."** Sub: *"Recoup is a research lab and implementation partner for labels, catalogs, and platforms — from strategy to the custom agents that run in your stack."*

Okay — "catalogs" is in the subhead, good, I'm in the right neighbourhood. "Custom agents that run in your stack" actually pings my ownership instinct a little. But the headline is generic-AI-vendor wallpaper; I've read that exact sentence on twenty sites this month. And the two buttons are **"Talk to us"** and **"Read our research"** — *neither* is "show me the thing." I came for a catalog plugin and the front door is a sales call. Slight deflation. I don't see the word "plugin," "valuation," "royalty," or "diligence" above the fold. 5-second verdict: probably relevant, but this smells like a consultancy, not a tool I can install. I'll scroll, but my guard is up.

## Section-by-section

### Header (chrome)
- **Thinking:** Three things: "Research" (opens a new tab), "Consulting," and a black "Talk to us" pill. That's it. Minimal.
- **Narrative so far:** Reinforces "this is a services firm." No "Product," no "Tools," no "Platform" up top — which is exactly what I'd hunt for.
- **Friction:** I came for a *thing to install*. The nav gives me two ways to "talk to a human" and one link off to a research blog. Where do I see the catalog plugin? Not here.
- **Works:** Clean, uncluttered, loads my eye to the centre. "Research" being prominent does flatter my "they actually know something" instinct.
- **Doesn't:** No product/tools entry point in the header. For a sceptic-of-SaaS who nonetheless wants to *try a tool*, the only paths are sales calls.

### 1. Hero
- **Thinking:** "...the custom agents that run in your stack" is the one phrase that holds me. "In your stack" hints I might keep it local/mine. But "research lab and implementation partner" = they'll want to be *in the room*, on retainer. I don't want a partner; I want a tool.
- **Narrative so far:** Premium, confident, consultancy-flavoured.
- **Friction:** Zero mention of my actual job: messy statements, income summaries, ballpark valuations. "Music business" is broad — I value catalogs, I don't *run* one.
- **Works:** Typography and polish signal competence. "Catalogs" named explicitly in the subhead.
- **Doesn't:** Both CTAs are top-of-funnel human contact. A "See the catalog tool" or "Try it on a statement" would have grabbed me instantly.

### 2. Logos — "Used by teams at"
- **Thinking:** Atlantic, Warner, 300, Rostrum, Fat Beats, ONErpm, **Duetti**, Big Ass Kids. Duetti and ONErpm jump out — Duetti literally buys song shares, ONErpm is distribution. So they touch the *commercial/catalog* side, not just artist marketing. That's a credibility bump.
- **Narrative so far:** "Real labels use this" — earns a bit of trust back.
- **Friction:** These are mostly *labels/distributors*, not *funds* or *valuation shops* like me. No catalog acquirer or financier logo I'd recognise as a peer (Duetti is the closest).
- **Works:** Recognisable names; "teams at" framing is honest enough.
- **Doesn't:** Doesn't speak to the buy-side/diligence world specifically. I can't tell if these are using the *tools* or buying *consulting*.

### 3. Lanes — "Research in public. Tools in the open. Implementation with your team."
- **Thinking:** Three cards: **Research** → "Read our research" (new tab); **Build** → "See the tools" → /platform; **Partner** → "Talk to us." Finally, "See the tools" — that's the one I want. The Build copy says *"Open skills, plugins, and an API so your team can put music intelligence into Claude, Cursor, and your own stack."* "Plugins" — that's the word from LinkedIn! And "open" + "your own stack" smells like the ownership I crave.
- **Narrative so far:** Now I understand the model: they publish research, give away tools, and sell hands-on help. The middle lane is mine.
- **Friction:** "skills," "Cursor," "API" — I'm a one-week-old Claude user. I know Claude. I don't know Cursor, I don't know what a "skill" is, and "API" makes me think "I'd need a developer." "Implementation with your team" again — I *am* the team.
- **Works:** The three-lane mental model is genuinely clear and well laid out. "See the tools" is the first CTA aimed at a self-server like me.
- **Doesn't:** The lane that fits me is wrapped in developer vocabulary that a brand-new Claude user can't parse.

### 4. Research — "We learn in the open, with real operators."
- **Thinking:** Copy says *"Catalog diligence, label operations, agent rollouts — we publish what actually works..."* **"Catalog diligence"** — that's literally my job, named. Good. Then the **ResearchCard** beside it shows... Gatsby Grace: monthly listeners, followers, saves, top cities, editorial playlists.
- **Narrative so far:** They *say* catalog diligence, but the visual proof is artist/marketing analytics.
- **Friction:** This is the core mismatch for me. I don't care about monthly listeners and top cities for a *valuation* — I care about **income over 3–4 years, top earning tracks, and vintage**. The card shows audience-growth data, which is A&R/marketing, not money. It quietly tells me their centre of gravity is artist marketing, not financial diligence.
- **Works:** "Catalog diligence" in the body copy keeps me reading. "We learn in the open, with real operators" lowers my generic-SaaS guard a notch.
- **Doesn't:** The supporting visual undercuts the diligence promise. Show me a normalised income summary, not a follower count.

### 5. Problem — "Your team has Claude. It still can't do the work."
- **Thinking:** Three gaps: *No music context* ("doesn't know your roster, catalog, or deal terms"), *No safe access* ("can't touch Drive, royalty data, or distributors on its own"), *No music workflows* ("Diligence, A&R, content ops — none of it built in"). Wait — **"royalty data"** and **"deal terms"** and **"Diligence"** are all here. This is the most *me* section so far.
- **Narrative so far:** They've correctly named that raw Claude can't do my job out of the box. I *have* Claude and I've already felt this — I tried pasting a statement in and it was useless without my context.
- **Friction:** "No safe access — it can't touch Drive, royalty data, or distributors on its own." This raises the privacy question loudly and then... doesn't answer it. *Whose* safe access? Does giving Recoup "safe access" mean my deal data leaves my machine? Nothing here reassures me on no-training/local.
- **Works:** Best-targeted copy on the page. "It still can't do the work" is exactly my week-one Claude experience.
- **Doesn't:** Names the privacy/access problem without resolving it. For someone who refuses to let a vendor train on her data, "safe access" is a question, not an answer.

### 6. Build (dark) — "One harness, many workflows."
- **Thinking:** Chips along the top: **Claude / Cursor / API / MCP / Chat**. Headline "One **harness**, many workflows." Then a demo cycling Terminal → Claude → ChatGPT showing `npm install -g @recoupable/cli`, `recoup research "Billie Eilish"`, and `recoup content create`.
- **Narrative so far:** This is where they lose me as a Claude beginner. I do not know what **"harness," "MCP," "Cursor,"** or a **CLI** is. The terminal screen — black box, `$` prompts, `npm install` — is exactly the thing I subscribed to Claude *to avoid touching*. I live in Excel.
- **Friction:** Two compounding problems. (1) **Jargon wall:** harness/MCP/CLI/Cursor are meaningless to me and make this feel like it's for engineers. (2) **Wrong demo:** the example researches Billie Eilish and generates *content* — "8 short-form videos, 3 covers, 1 press kit." That's marketing output. I'm valuing catalogs; I'd want to see `recoup` ingest three messy royalty CSVs and spit out a clean income summary. The sub-copy even says *"Chat is a hosted workspace"* — **hosted** is the opposite of what I want.
- **Works:** Visually impressive; the multi-surface (Claude/ChatGPT/terminal) idea hints "use it where you already are." The "bring your own agent, Recoup plugs in" line is, in principle, the ownership story I want — but it's buried.
- **Doesn't:** Peak jargon + a content demo + "hosted workspace" = this section actively argues *against* my use case. A beginner Excel valuer reads this and concludes "not for me."

### 7. Open tools shelf — "The tools we build, free to install."
- **Thinking:** Five "books" on a shelf; the first is **Agentic Diligence** — *"Skills, agents, and templates for catalog audits and deal rooms."* `npx skills add recoupable/diligence`. **This is the thing.** "Catalog audits and deal rooms" is my world. "Free to install" and "open source" gesture at ownership.
- **Narrative so far:** After the content-heavy detour, this pulls me back. The Diligence pack is exactly what the LinkedIn post promised.
- **Friction:** But: (1) It's not a link. I can't *click* "Agentic Diligence" to learn what's actually in it — there's only an install command. (2) The command is `npx skills add recoupable/diligence` — `npx`? I have no idea what that is or where I'd type it. As a one-week Claude user, this is a dead end; I can't action it. (3) "Skills," "agents," "templates" — three undefined words. (4) Still nothing about *my* data staying private/local, even though "open source" implies I could run it myself.
- **Works:** The pack *titles and one-liners* are the sharpest targeting on the whole page. "Catalog audits and deal rooms" made me lean in. The shelf interaction is delightful.
- **Doesn't:** The single most relevant item on the site is un-clickable and un-installable for the exact beginner the hero invited in. No "how to install" for non-developers, no detail page, no sample output.

### 8. Consulting band — "Most music teams are still planning their AI strategy. Start executing it."
- **Thinking:** *"Training and implementation from operators who build the tools — not slide decks."* CTA "Talk to us" → /consulting.
- **Narrative so far:** Back to selling me consulting. Reasonable, but I don't want training; I want a tool I can run tonight.
- **Friction:** "Most music *teams*" — solo again. And this is a retainer/services pitch, which is the high-cost, high-touch path. I wanted the low-touch, self-serve, own-it path.
- **Works:** "operators who build the tools — not slide decks" is a good differentiator if I *were* shopping for consulting.
- **Doesn't:** Doesn't serve the "I'll try it myself first" buyer. No middle option between "install a cryptic CLI command" and "book a consultant."

### 9. Pull quote — "Catalog fund operator"
- **Thinking:** *"Catalog diligence is one of the biggest pain points I have. Cut it down to minutes and it changes how we buy."* — **Catalog fund operator.** That's... me. That's my exact sentence. My client says this monthly.
- **Narrative so far:** Emotionally, this is the strongest beat on the page for my persona. Someone like me said the thing I feel.
- **Friction:** It's anonymous and there's *no link* to a case study, a number, or a "here's how we cut it to minutes." So it reads as aspirational copy, not proof. "Cut it down to minutes" — *how?* Show me. The claim is exactly what I want to believe and exactly what I most need evidence for, and there's none attached.
- **Works:** Perfect voice-of-customer match. If it linked to a real before/after on royalty-statement normalisation, I'd be sold.
- **Doesn't:** No proof behind the most important promise. A pull quote can't carry a six-figure-decision claim alone.

### 10. Proof — "We run our own labels."
- **Thinking:** Recoup Records + artist Gatsby Grace use the tools to "write release plans, audit catalogs, pitch playlists, run the business." CTA "See how we dogfood" → /company/recoup-records.
- **Narrative so far:** Dogfooding is a fair trust signal — they use their own kit.
- **Friction:** It's *artist/label operations* proof, not *buy-side diligence* proof. "Audit catalogs" is in the list, but it's one of four marketing-flavoured bullets. A tiny label running its own roster is a very different animal from a fund screening 140 catalogs a year. Doesn't prove they handle *my* messy-statement, multi-seller reality.
- **Works:** "Every skill we publish earns its keep on a real roster first" is a nice integrity line.
- **Doesn't:** Wrong proof for the buy-side. I need "we ran diligence on an acquisition," not "we promoted our artist."

### 11. Final CTA — "Let's build it in your stack."
- **Thinking:** Two buttons again: "Talk to us" → /consulting, "Read our research" → research.
- **Narrative so far:** We end exactly where we started: book a call or read the blog.
- **Friction:** After eleven sections, I *still* have no self-serve way to try the Diligence tool, and no privacy/ownership reassurance. "Build it in your stack" is the right phrase — but the only door is a sales call.
- **Works:** "in your stack" is on-message for ownership.
- **Doesn't:** No "Install Agentic Diligence" or "Try it on a sample statement" CTA. The whole page funnels a self-server into a human-sales motion.

### Footer (chrome)
- **Thinking:** Now I find more: **Build → Platform / Developers / API Docs / Pricing.** Aha — there *is* a Pricing page and a Platform page, just hidden down here.
- **Narrative so far:** The things a buyer evaluates (pricing, platform, developers) were in the basement the whole time.
- **Friction:** I hunted for "what does it cost / what is the product" the entire scroll, and it was footer-only. "Developers" and "API Docs" again confirm this leans engineer. "Recoup Records" here also duplicates the /company/recoup-records I already saw.
- **Works:** Complete; Pricing and Platform at least *exist*.
- **Doesn't:** Burying Platform + Pricing penalises the self-serve evaluator. No "Catalog / Diligence" footer link either — the one thing I came for has no home page.

## Objections that would make me bounce

1. **"Where is the catalog plugin I came for?"** It has no page — only an un-clickable install line. The promise that brought me has no destination.
2. **Jargon I can't parse one week into Claude:** "harness," "MCP," "Cursor," "CLI," "skills," "plugins," "npx." The Build section reads as built for engineers, not for me.
3. **The product demos are content/marketing (Billie Eilish videos, follower counts), not money** — income, top-earning tracks, vintage, statement normalisation. Wrong centre of gravity for valuation.
4. **"Hosted workspace" / "Get started → hosted app"** collides head-on with my "I want to own it locally and you don't see my data" requirement.
5. **No privacy/no-training reassurance anywhere**, despite the page literally raising "safe access… royalty data."
6. **It reads as a consultancy** (Talk to us × 4, $500 sessions) when I wanted a self-serve tool to try tonight.
7. **Nothing in my language:** no Excel, Power BI, CSV, royalty statement, DCF, or "first-pass screen."

## Verdict — would I convert?

**Likelihood: 3/10.**

I would not book a call and I could not install the tool, so on this visit I bounce — but I don't write them off. Three beats kept me reading past the jargon wall: the Problem section ("royalty data… deal terms… Diligence"), the **Agentic Diligence** pack one-liner ("catalog audits and deal rooms"), and the **"Catalog fund operator"** pull quote that is word-for-word my pain. That's real signal that *someone here understands buy-side diligence.* But the page is positioned for label-marketing teams and engineers, funnels everything into a sales call or a hosted app, demos content instead of income, never answers my data-privacy/ownership question, and makes the one tool I want un-clickable and un-installable for a Claude beginner. I'd probably DM the LinkedIn poster asking "is there a page for the catalog thing?" rather than convert here.

## Top 5 fixes (ranked)

1. **[Conversion] Give "Agentic Diligence" a real destination + a beginner install path.** Make the shelf pack click through to a `/diligence` (or `/catalog`) page with sample output (a normalised 3–4-year income summary, top tracks, vintage), and a "How to install — no terminal required" walkthrough for brand-new Claude users. Right now the highest-intent item on the page is a dead end.
2. **[Positioning] Add a self-serve lane between "install a CLI" and "book a call."** A "Try it on a sample royalty statement" interactive demo (or a one-click Claude install) so a sceptic-of-SaaS who wants to test it herself has a path that isn't a $500 consulting session.
3. **[Clarity] De-jargon the Build section for non-engineers.** "Harness," "MCP," "Cursor," "CLI," "npx" lose a one-week Claude user. Lead with plain language ("Add Recoup's catalog tools to the Claude you already have") and keep the developer vocabulary in a clearly-labelled "for developers" sub-area.
4. **[Conversion/Trust] Answer the data question explicitly.** A visible "Your data stays yours — runs in your stack, we never train on it" line near the Problem ("safe access") and Build sections. For buy-side diligence, this is a gating concern, not a nice-to-have.
5. **[Positioning] Make the diligence demo about *money*, not content.** Swap (or add) a buy-side example: ingest messy multi-format royalty statements → clean income-by-year, top-earning tracks, vintage, ballpark. And back the "cut it to minutes" pull quote with a real before/after, ideally Excel/Power BI-friendly output, since that's where this buyer actually works.
